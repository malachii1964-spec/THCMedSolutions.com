Your buds are dried. Stems snap. The outside feels dry to the touch.
Now comes the part that transforms good weed into great weed: the cure.

Curing is a controlled aging process where residual moisture inside
the bud slowly migrates outward, chlorophyll continues to break down,
and terpenes undergo chemical changes that deepen aroma and smoothness.
Uncured cannabis is smokable. Properly cured cannabis is enjoyable.

## Why curing matters

### The chemistry

When you dry cannabis, you remove the bulk of the water — from 75% down
to 10–12%. But drying doesn't complete the chemical processes that
determine smoke quality:

- **Chlorophyll breakdown** — chlorophyll gives plants their green color
  and makes smoke harsh and "grassy." It breaks down slowly over weeks.
  A proper cure gives it time to degrade fully
- **Terpene maturation** — some terpene precursors convert to their
  final aromatic form during curing. This is why cured cannabis smells
  different (and better) than freshly dried cannabis
- **Starch and sugar conversion** — residual starches and sugars in
  plant tissue break down during curing. These contribute to harshness
  when combusted
- **Moisture equalization** — even after a good dry, the inside of the
  bud holds more moisture than the outside. Curing slowly equalizes
  this, preventing the "dry outside, wet inside" problem that leads to
  mold in storage

### The practical difference

| Quality | Uncured (dried only) | 2-week cure | 4+ week cure |
|---------|---------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Smell | Hay/grass | Emerging terpenes | Full strain aroma |
| Taste | Harsh, green | Smoother, some flavor | Smooth, complex |
| Smoke | Hot, irritating | Acceptable | Clean white ash |
| Potency | Full (THC present) | Full | Full (slightly better bioavailability) |
| Shelf life | 1–2 months before degradation | 3–4 months | 6–12+ months |

## The jar-curing protocol

### What you need

- **Mason jars** (wide-mouth quart jars are the standard). Clear glass
  so you can see the buds. One quart jar holds roughly 1 ounce of dried,
  untrimmed bud
- **Hygrometer** — small round hygrometers that fit inside the jar lid.
  Caliber IV or similar ($5–8 each). You need one per jar (or at least
  one per batch)
- **Boveda or Integra Boost packs** — 62% RH two-way humidity control
  packs (optional but recommended). These absorb moisture when humidity
  is too high and release it when too low
- **Dark storage space** — a closet shelf, a cabinet, a drawer. Anywhere
  dark and room-temperature

### Step 1: Jar the buds

After drying (stems snap, buds feel dry outside with slight give when
squeezed), trim if you haven't already (dry trim), then fill jars:

- **Fill to 75% capacity** — don't pack tightly. Leave room for air
  exchange when you open the jar. Buds should move freely when you
  shake the jar gently
- **Don't break up buds** — leave them as intact as possible. Smaller
  surface area = slower moisture loss = better cure
- **Place a hygrometer inside** — set it on top of the buds, close
  the lid, wait 4–6 hours. The reading after this stabilization period
  is your actual bud moisture level

### Step 2: Read the hygrometer

After 4–6 hours sealed, check the reading:

| Jar RH | What it means | Action |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Below 55% | Over-dried | Add a Boveda 62% pack. Some moisture can be recovered, but severely over-dried buds never fully cure |
| 55–58% | Slightly dry | Add a Boveda 62% pack. Cure normally — the pack will slowly rehydrate to the right range |
| 58–62% | Perfect | Cure as described below. This is the sweet spot |
| 62–65% | Slightly too moist | Burp more aggressively (see below). Leave jars open for 30–60 minutes per session |
| 65–70% | Too moist | Take buds out of jars, spread on a drying rack for 12–24 hours, re-jar and check again |
| Above 70% | Mold risk | Buds are not dry enough to jar. Return to drying for 1–2 days, then re-jar |

### Step 3: The burping schedule

"Burping" is opening the jar to exchange stale, moisture-laden air with
fresh air. This is how moisture slowly leaves the buds during the cure.

**Week 1 (days 1–7):**
- Open each jar 2–3 times per day for 10–15 minutes
- Gently shake the buds or turn the jar to prevent clumping
- If buds feel damp or sticky, leave the jar open for 30 minutes
- If you smell ammonia (sour, fermented odor), buds are too wet —
  take them out immediately and dry for another 12–24 hours. Ammonia
  means anaerobic bacteria are active, which destroys the bud

**Week 2 (days 8–14):**
- Open each jar once per day for 10–15 minutes
- The hay/grass smell should be fading, replaced by emerging terpene
  aromas
- Humidity should be stabilizing in the 58–62% range

**Weeks 3–4:**
- Open jars every 2–3 days for 5–10 minutes
- Aroma should be developing noticeably — you'll start smelling the
  strain's characteristic terpenes
- The smoke should be noticeably smoother than week 1

**Month 2+:**
- Open jars once a week for a few minutes
- After 4–6 weeks, the cure is functionally complete for most purposes
- You can continue curing for months — quality continues to improve
  (slowly) up to about 6 months for most strains

### Step 4: The long cure (optional, for peak quality)

Some strains — especially those with complex terpene profiles — benefit
from extended curing of 2–6 months. During this period:

- Burp once a week or less
- Keep a Boveda 62% pack in each jar for consistent humidity
- Store in complete darkness at 60–70°F
- The improvement from month 1 to month 3 is significant. From month
  3 to month 6, it's subtle but noticeable to experienced smokers

After 6 months, quality plateaus. Beyond 12 months, THC slowly degrades
to CBN (more sedative, less psychoactive) — still good, but different.

## Boveda packs: yes or no?

**Boveda (or Integra Boost) 62% packs** are two-way humidity control
sachets. They maintain 62% RH inside the jar by absorbing excess
moisture or releasing moisture as needed.

### The case for them

- Set and forget humidity control
- Insurance against over-drying during long storage
- Consistent results even in variable storage environments
- Cheap (~$1 per pack, each lasts 2–4 months)

### The case against them

Some growers report that Boveda packs slightly mute terpene expression
— the theory being that the packs absorb some volatile terpenes along
with the moisture. This is debated:

- A 2020 study found no significant terpene difference between
  Boveda-cured and manually-cured samples
- Anecdotally, some premium growers and connoisseurs prefer manual
  burping without packs
- The difference, if any, is subtle enough that most consumers can't
  detect it

**The recommendation:** use Boveda packs. The protection against
over-drying and the consistency they provide outweigh any theoretical
terpene loss. If you're curing competition-grade flower and have the
discipline to burp precisely, try without packs and compare.

## Long-term storage

After curing is complete (4+ weeks minimum, 8+ weeks ideal), you can
store for months:

### The ideal storage conditions

| Factor | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Humidity | 58–62% RH | Too dry = brittle trichomes. Too wet = mold |
| Temperature | 60–70°F | Heat degrades THC. Cool is better |
| Light | Zero | UV converts THC to CBN, degrades terpenes |
| Air | Minimal headspace in jar | Oxygen degrades cannabinoids over time |
| Container | Glass (Mason jars) | Plastic can leach chemicals and create static that pulls trichomes off buds |

### Storage mistakes that ruin cured bud

1. **Plastic bags** — static clings to trichomes. Opening a plastic
   bag of cured bud leaves a visible coating of kief on the inside of
   the bag. That's potency you're losing. Always use glass
2. **The freezer debate** — freezing preserves THC well but makes
   trichomes extremely brittle. Any handling of frozen buds shatters
   trichome heads. If you freeze, don't touch the buds until they've
   returned to room temperature. And never re-freeze after thawing
3. **Leaving jars in light** — a jar on a sunny shelf degrades faster
   than a jar in a dark closet. If your jars are clear glass, store
   them in a dark cabinet or use UV-blocking jars (Infinity Jars, or
   wrap in foil)
4. **Opening jars frequently** — every time you open a jar, you exchange
   humidity-controlled air for ambient air. If you're dipping into your
   stash daily, transfer a week's supply to a smaller jar and keep the
   main jar sealed

## Troubleshooting the cure

### "My buds smell like hay"

This is normal in the first 1–2 weeks of curing. The hay smell is
chlorophyll breaking down. It fades as the cure progresses. If it
persists past week 3, the buds were dried too fast — a Boveda pack
and extended curing (6–8 weeks) can partially recover terpene expression,
but some damage is permanent.

### "My buds smell like ammonia"

Wet buds in a sealed jar. Anaerobic bacteria are consuming the plant
tissue. **Take the buds out immediately** and spread them on a drying
rack for 24 hours. Re-jar when they feel dry again. Check for mold —
if any buds show gray or white fuzz, discard them. Ammonia-damaged
buds can sometimes be salvaged for edibles but not for smoking.

### "My buds are too dry and crumbly"

Over-dried. Recovery options:
1. Add a Boveda 62% pack and seal the jar. Over 5–7 days, the buds
   will rehydrate slightly
2. Place a small piece of fresh lettuce or a tortilla in the jar for
   2–4 hours (remove after — don't leave organic material in long-term).
   This is a quick-fix for immediate rehydration, not a substitute for
   Boveda
3. Over-dried buds can still be cured, but the chlorophyll breakdown
   may have been interrupted. Extended curing (6–8 weeks) helps, but
   they'll never reach the quality of properly dried buds

### "I found mold in a jar"

Discard the affected buds immediately. Check all other buds in the same
jar — mold spores spread invisibly. If the mold is confined to one or
two buds, the rest may be salvageable if they look and smell clean.
When in doubt, throw it out — smoking moldy cannabis is a genuine
health risk.

Reduce humidity: leave jars open for 30–60 minutes, add a desiccant
briefly if needed, and check your storage environment. Mold during
curing almost always means the buds were too wet when jarred.

## The minimum cure

Don't have the patience for a 4-week cure? The minimum that makes a
noticeable difference:

- **7 days** of proper burping produces noticeably smoother smoke than
  uncured bud
- **14 days** is where most of the chlorophyll breakdown completes and
  the "green" taste disappears
- **21 days** is where terpene complexity starts to emerge

If you can't wait 4 weeks, wait at least 2. The difference between
day-1 dried bud and day-14 cured bud is dramatic.
