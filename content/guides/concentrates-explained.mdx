You've grown great flower. You've dried and cured it properly. Now
you have options beyond smoking buds — concentrates take the best parts
of the flower (trichomes, cannabinoids, terpenes) and remove
everything else (plant matter, chlorophyll, wax). The result is a
product that's 2–4× more potent than flower, with flavors that can
be more intense and complex than the source material.

This guide is a grower's overview of every major concentrate type:
what it is, how it's made, whether you can make it at home, and what
separates good concentrate from mediocre.

## The concentrate spectrum

| Type | Potency (THC) | Terpenes | Home-makeable? | Method |
|------|-------------|----------|---------------|--------|
| Kief | 30–60% | Low-moderate | Yes (easy) | Mechanical separation |
| Dry sift hash | 40–70% | Moderate | Yes (easy) | Screen sieving |
| Bubble hash | 40–80% | High | Yes (moderate) | Ice water extraction |
| Flower rosin | 60–80% | High | Yes (easy) | Heat + pressure |
| Hash rosin | 70–90% | Very high | Yes (moderate) | Bubble hash → heat + pressure |
| Live rosin | 70–90% | Highest | Yes (advanced) | Fresh frozen → bubble hash → press |
| BHO (shatter/wax) | 70–90% | Variable | No (dangerous) | Butane extraction |
| Live resin | 70–90% | Very high | No (specialized equipment) | Butane + fresh frozen |
| Distillate | 85–99% | None (added back) | No | Distillation |
| Diamonds/sauce | 80–99% | Varies | No | THCA crystallization |

## Solventless concentrates (home-grower friendly)

### Kief

**What it is:** the trichome heads that fall off cannabis when it's
ground, handled, or sieved. The fine powder that collects in the bottom
chamber of a grinder is kief.

**How to collect it:**
- Use a grinder with a kief catcher (the bottom screen chamber)
- Store trim in the freezer and shake it in a bag — kief falls off
  frozen trichomes easily
- Run trim over a 120µ screen

**Quality:** kief is the lowest-quality concentrate because it's
contaminated with plant material. The typical kief from a grinder is
30–50% cannabinoids. It's still a significant potency boost when
sprinkled on flower.

### Dry sift

**What it is:** a refined version of kief. Instead of passively
collecting what falls off, you actively sieve dried cannabis through
progressively finer screens to isolate trichome heads.

**Quality:** proper multi-pass dry sift (120µ → 90µ → 70µ) produces
50–70% cannabinoid content. With static tech cleaning, it can reach
near-full-melt quality. See our
[bubble hash and dry sift guide](/guides/bubble-hash-dry-sift).

### Bubble hash (ice water hash)

**What it is:** cannabis trichomes extracted using ice water and mesh
bags. The cold makes trichome stalks brittle; agitation snaps them
off; the trichome heads are collected by size through a series of
screens.

**Quality:** the 73µ and 90µ fractions of properly made bubble hash
from quality starting material can reach "full melt" (6-star) —
meaning it melts completely when dabbed with zero residue. This is
premium solventless concentrate.

**Home-makeable:** yes, with a bubble bag set ($30–80) and ice. See
our [bubble hash guide](/guides/bubble-hash-dry-sift).

### Flower rosin

**What it is:** concentrate extracted from cannabis flower using only
heat and pressure. A rosin press squeezes a bud between heated plates,
and the resin flows out as a translucent, terpene-rich oil.

**How to make it at home:**
1. Take a dense, frosty bud (0.5–2g)
2. Place it in a rosin bag (25µ or 37µ mesh)
3. Press between the plates of a rosin press at 180–220°F for 45–90
   seconds
4. The oil that squeezes out is flower rosin — collect it on parchment
   paper

**Equipment:**
- A dedicated rosin press ($200–500 for a quality home unit — Dabpress,
  NugSmasher, Rosineer)
- A hair straightener works for experimentation ($15) but produces
  inconsistent results and low yield
- Rosin bags ($10 for 50+)

**Quality:** flower rosin is 60–80% cannabinoids with excellent terpene
preservation (the low temperature preserves volatile terpenes). Quality
depends heavily on the starting material — frosty, resinous flower
produces the best rosin.

**Yield:** typically 15–25% return (1g of bud produces 0.15–0.25g of
rosin). Higher for very resinous strains.

See our [rosin pressing guide](/guides/pressing-rosin-at-home).

### Hash rosin

**What it is:** rosin pressed from bubble hash instead of flower. The
starting material is already purified trichomes, so the result is
cleaner, more potent, and more flavorful than flower rosin.

**The process:**
1. Make bubble hash from fresh frozen or dried material
2. Dry the hash thoroughly
3. Press the hash in a rosin bag at low temperature (160–190°F)
4. Collect the output — this is hash rosin

**Quality:** hash rosin from quality starting material is the pinnacle
of solventless concentration. 70–90% cannabinoids with the full terpene
profile preserved. This is what wins cannabis cups in the solventless
category.

### Live rosin

**What it is:** hash rosin made from fresh-frozen starting material
(plant material frozen immediately after harvest, never dried). "Live"
means the terpene profile is as close to the living plant as possible
— no terpene loss from drying.

**The process:**
1. Harvest the plant → immediately freeze (within 2 hours)
2. Make bubble hash from the fresh-frozen material
3. Freeze-dry the bubble hash (or microplane and air-dry)
4. Press the hash into rosin at low temperature

**Quality:** live rosin is the highest-quality cannabis concentrate
available. It preserves terpenes that evaporate during drying, captures
the full flavor and aroma of the living plant, and contains no
solvents. It's also the most expensive concentrate on the market
($60–100/gram retail).

**For home growers:** this is absolutely achievable, but requires
quality starting material, a bubble bag set, and a rosin press. The
investment pays off if you're growing resinous strains.

## Solvent-based concentrates (not for home production)

These concentrates use chemical solvents (butane, propane, ethanol, or
CO₂) to dissolve trichomes from plant material, then the solvent is
purged. They're produced commercially in licensed labs with safety
equipment. **Do not attempt butane or propane extraction at home.**

### BHO: shatter, wax, budder, crumble

**What it is:** butane hash oil — cannabis extracted using liquid
butane. Different post-processing techniques produce different
textures:

| Texture | How it's made | Characteristics |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Shatter | Thin spread, slow purge, no agitation | Glass-like, translucent, snaps when cold |
| Wax | Whipped during purge | Opaque, creamy, easy to handle |
| Budder | Whipped at higher temperatures | Smooth, butter-like consistency |
| Crumble | Low-temp vacuum purge | Dry, crumbly, honeycomb texture |
| Sugar | Partial crystallization during purge | Granular, wet-looking, terpene-rich |

**Quality:** 70–90% cannabinoids. Terpene preservation varies — lower
processing temperatures preserve more terpenes.

### Live resin

**What it is:** BHO made from fresh-frozen plant material (never
dried). The "live" designation means the same thing as in live rosin —
maximum terpene preservation.

**Quality:** live resin is one of the most flavorful concentrate types.
The butane extraction captures a broader terpene profile than most
solventless methods, and the fresh-frozen starting material preserves
volatile terpenes that drying would destroy.

**How it compares to live rosin:** both use fresh-frozen material and
produce exceptional terpene profiles. Live resin uses butane; live
rosin uses only heat and pressure. Live rosin commands a premium because
it's solventless.

### Distillate

**What it is:** cannabis oil refined through short-path distillation
to isolate specific cannabinoids (usually THC or CBD). The result is
a nearly pure cannabinoid oil — 85–99% THC with almost no terpenes,
flavonoids, or other plant compounds.

**Characteristics:** distillate is odorless, flavorless, and very
potent. It's the base ingredient in most commercial vape cartridges
and edibles. Manufacturers add terpenes back (either cannabis-derived
or botanical) for flavor in vape products.

**The trade-off:** maximum potency but minimal entourage effect.
Distillate feels different from full-spectrum concentrates because
it's missing the terpene and minor cannabinoid synergy that shapes
the experience.

### Diamonds and sauce

**What it is:** THCA crystalline (the "diamonds") suspended in a
terpene-rich liquid (the "sauce"). Made through a controlled
crystallization process where THCA precipitates out of a supersaturated
solution.

**Quality:** diamonds can be 95–99% THCA. The sauce surrounding them
is a concentrated terpene extract. Together, they provide extreme
potency with excellent flavor.

## What to grow for concentrates

Not all cannabis makes great concentrate. What you want in concentrate
starting material:

- **Dense trichome coverage** — the more resin, the higher the yield
- **Large trichome heads** — bigger heads = more cannabinoids per
  trichome = better extraction efficiency
- **Strong terpene profile** — concentrates amplify terpenes. A strain
  that smells average as flower smells average-times-ten as concentrate
- **Dense, resinous buds** — airy, leafy buds produce lower yields
  and more plant contamination

**Good concentrate strains:** Gorilla Glue (GG4), Chemdawg, GMO,
Wedding Cake, Ice Cream Cake, Papaya, any "cookie" cross

## Quality indicators

How to tell if a concentrate is good (whether you made it or bought
it):

| Indicator | Good | Bad |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Color | Light gold to amber (solventless), light gold (BHO) | Dark brown or green (plant contamination) |
| Clarity | Translucent or uniform opacity | Cloudy, uneven, or with dark particulates |
| Smell | Strong, terpene-rich, identifiable strain aroma | Chemical smell, no aroma, or "burnt" odor |
| Taste | Clean, flavorful, smooth | Harsh, chemical, "solvent" taste |
| Residue | Minimal (full melt = zero residue when dabbed) | Heavy black residue on the banger = plant contamination |
| Consistency | Stable — doesn't change texture at room temp | Auto-buddering, excessive moisture, stickiness |
