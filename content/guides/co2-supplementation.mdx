Cannabis uses CO₂ for photosynthesis. More CO₂ = more photosynthesis = more growth — **but only if the light, temperature, and nutrients can keep up.** CO₂ without matching intensity is wasted money.

## The CO₂ hierarchy of needs

CO₂ supplementation is the **last** optimization, not the first. In order:

1. ✅ Light (adequate PPFD for your canopy)
2. ✅ Temperature and humidity (VPD dialed)
3. ✅ Nutrients and pH (feeding correctly)
4. ✅ Airflow and pest prevention
5. ✅ Training and canopy management
6. → **Then** CO₂ supplementation

If items 1–5 aren't locked, CO₂ won't help and will add complexity to a system that isn't ready.

## The numbers

| CO₂ Level | Source | Growth rate |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| ~420 ppm | Ambient outdoor air | Baseline |
| ~300 ppm | Sealed room without supplementation | **Below** baseline (plants consumed it) |
| 800–1000 ppm | Light supplementation | ~20–30% faster growth |
| 1200–1500 ppm | Full supplementation | ~30–50% faster growth |
| >1500 ppm | Diminishing returns | Minimal extra benefit, toxic at >3000 |

> A well-ventilated tent exchanging air every 1–3 minutes maintains ~400 ppm naturally. That's the **baseline** — and it's enough for most home grows.

## When CO₂ makes sense

- Your light is already at **800+ PPFD** (plants can't use the extra CO₂ at low light)
- Your room is **sealed** (CO₂ escapes through exhaust fans — if you're exhausting, you're venting your CO₂)
- Temps are **80–85 °F** (CO₂-supplemented plants tolerate and prefer higher temps)
- You've already maxed out every other variable

## When CO₂ is a waste

- Your tent has an exhaust fan running (it pulls CO₂ out as fast as you add it)
- Your light is under 600 PPFD (the plant can't photosynthesize fast enough to use it)
- You're growing 1–4 plants as a hobby (the investment rarely pays back at small scale)
- Your environment isn't stable yet (fix the basics first)

## Methods of CO₂ supplementation

### 1. Compressed CO₂ tanks
- **How:** Regulator + timer + solenoid valve releases CO₂ at set intervals.
- **Cost:** ~$200–400 setup, ~$30–50 per tank refill.
- **Precision:** High — you can dial exact PPM with a controller.
- **Best for:** Sealed rooms, serious growers.

### 2. CO₂ generators (burners)
- **How:** Burns propane or natural gas to produce CO₂.
- **Cost:** ~$150–300 for the generator, ongoing fuel.
- **Precision:** Moderate — also produces heat and water vapor.
- **Best for:** Large sealed rooms where the extra heat/humidity is manageable.
- **Caution:** Open flame, combustion byproducts, not for small spaces.

### 3. CO₂ bags (mycelium/mushroom bags)
- **How:** Fungi produce CO₂ as they grow.
- **Cost:** ~$20–40 per bag, lasts ~6 months.
- **Precision:** Very low — produces maybe 50–100 ppm increase in a small tent.
- **Best for:** Nothing, honestly. The output is too low to measure, too inconsistent to rely on.

> CO₂ bags are the #1 "waste of money" item in cannabis growing. The math doesn't work for any tent setup. Save the $30.

### 4. Dry ice
- **How:** Dry ice (solid CO₂) sublimates, releasing CO₂ gas.
- **Cost:** Cheap but inconvenient — needs daily replacement.
- **Precision:** None — uncontrolled release.
- **Best for:** Experimentation only.

## The sealed-room setup

To actually benefit from CO₂:

1. **Seal the room.** No exhaust fan, no intake fan.
2. **Run a mini-split AC** for cooling (no air exchange = no passive cooling).
3. **Run a dehumidifier** (no exhaust = humidity builds up).
4. **Use a CO₂ controller** (sensor + solenoid) to maintain target PPM.
5. **Raise lights to 1000+ PPFD** and temps to 80–85 °F.

This is a fundamentally different grow setup than a standard exhaust-cooled tent. Don't bolt CO₂ onto a tent setup — it won't work.

## Safety

- CO₂ is heavier than air and pools at floor level.
- At 3000+ ppm it causes headaches; at 40,000+ ppm it's lethal.
- **Always have a CO₂ monitor** in any supplemented space.
- Turn off CO₂ during lights-off (plants don't photosynthesize in the dark).
- Ensure the space is well-ventilated when you enter it.

## The honest take

For 90% of home growers: **skip CO₂ and spend the money on a better light.** A $200 CO₂ tank setup in a ventilated tent does essentially nothing. Proper exhaust bringing in fresh 420 ppm air is more effective than trying to supplement in a leaky environment.

CO₂ is for sealed rooms with 1000+ PPFD lights, AC, and dehumidification — a setup that costs thousands. If that's you, it's the final unlock. If that's not you, it's the final waste of money.
