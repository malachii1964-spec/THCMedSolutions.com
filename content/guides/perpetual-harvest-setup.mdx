A perpetual harvest means running plants in different stages simultaneously
so there's always something ready to cut. Instead of one big harvest followed
by months of waiting, you pull down a plant (or a few) every 2–4 weeks. It's
how commercial grows operate, and home growers can do it on a smaller scale.

## The core concept

A single photoperiod plant takes roughly 4 months from seed/clone to harvest
(4 weeks veg + 8 weeks flower + 2 weeks dry/cure). In a perpetual setup,
you stagger multiple plants so when one finishes flower, the next is entering
flower, the one after that is in mid-veg, and the newest is just rooting.

**The result:** continuous, predictable harvests. No feast-or-famine cycles.

## What you need

### Minimum: two separate light zones

You cannot run perpetual with one light on one timer. Veg plants need 18/6
(or 20/4) while flowering plants need 12/12. You need at least two isolated
spaces:

| Zone | Light cycle | Purpose |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Veg chamber | 18/6 or 20/4 | Seedlings, clones, veg plants |
| Flower tent | 12/12 | Plants in bloom |

A common setup:

- **Flower tent:** 4×4 or 4×2 (your main light, your best environment)
- **Veg space:** 2×2 tent, shelf with a grow light, closet with a T5

The veg space doesn't need to be fancy — plants in veg are forgiving. The
flower tent is where you invest.

### Optional: a drying/curing area

If you're pulling plants every 2–4 weeks, you need a consistent drying
space. A separate small tent (2×2) with a small fan and no light works
well. Or hang them in a closet at 60°F/60% humidity.

## The timing math

### Photoperiods

Assuming 8-week flower and 4-week veg:

| Week | Plant A | Plant B | Plant C | Plant D |
|------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Flower W1 | Veg W4 | Veg W1 | Clone/seed |
| 2 | Flower W2 | Flower W1 | Veg W2 | Veg W1 |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| 8 | **Harvest** | Flower W7 | Flower W4 | Veg W4 |
| 9 | New clone | **Harvest** | Flower W5 | Flower W1 |

With 4 plants staggered 2 weeks apart, you harvest one plant every 2 weeks.

**Scaling:**

- 2 plants, 4 weeks apart → harvest every 4 weeks
- 4 plants, 2 weeks apart → harvest every 2 weeks
- 6 plants, 10 days apart → harvest every 10 days

### Autoflowers

Autoflowers make perpetual even simpler — no separate light zones needed.
Run them all on 20/4 in the same tent. Start a new seed every 2–3 weeks.
By the time the oldest is harvested, the newest is in mid-veg.

**Autoflower perpetual in a 4×4 tent:**

- Start 1 auto every 2–3 weeks
- Run 3–5 plants at staggered stages in the same tent
- All under 20/4 light
- Harvest the oldest every 2–3 weeks

The downside: autoflower yields are typically smaller per plant, so you need
more of them to match photoperiod harvests.

## Cloning: the engine of perpetual

Perpetual runs best on clones, not seeds. Here's why:

1. **Consistency** — every clone is genetically identical, so you know
   exactly how it grows, feeds, and finishes
2. **Speed** — clones skip the seedling stage (saves 2–3 weeks vs. seed)
3. **Cost** — seeds are $5–15 each; clones are free after the mother plant
4. **No males** — clones from a female are always female

**Mother plant approach:** keep one plant in permanent veg (never flip it).
Take 2–4 cuttings every 2–3 weeks. Those clones become your next flower
plants. One mother can supply perpetual harvest indefinitely.

See our [cloning guide](/guides/cloning-and-propagation) for the technique.

## Space planning

### Small perpetual (closet/bedroom)

- 1 × 4×2 flower tent (2 plants flowering at a time)
- 1 × 2×2 veg tent (2 veg plants + mother)
- Harvest: 1 plant every 4 weeks
- Annual yield: ~12–16 oz (with good genetics and technique)

### Medium perpetual (dedicated room)

- 1 × 4×4 flower tent (4 plants flowering)
- 1 × 3×3 veg tent (4 veg plants + 1–2 mothers)
- Harvest: 1 plant every 2 weeks
- Annual yield: ~24–48 oz

### Large perpetual (two rooms)

- Dedicated flower room (8+ plants)
- Dedicated veg room (clones, mothers, veg)
- Harvest: multiple plants weekly
- This is commercial-scale home growing

## Common perpetual mistakes

### 1. Not enough veg space

Growers invest in the flower tent and forget about veg. If your clones
aren't ready when a spot opens in the flower tent, you lose weeks of
production. Oversupply veg to guarantee a replacement is always ready.

### 2. Pest carryover

When you never fully empty and clean the flower tent, pests persist from
cycle to cycle. Mitigate with strong IPM (see our
[IPM guide](/guides/ipm-integrated-pest-management)) and clean individual
plants thoroughly before moving them to flower.

### 3. Running too many strains

Different strains finish at different times, feed differently, and grow
to different heights. Starting with 1–2 strains simplifies everything.
Add variety once the rhythm is locked in.

### 4. Forgetting the dry space

If you harvest every 2 weeks but drying takes 10–14 days, you need a
separate drying area that's always available. Plan for this upfront.

## Getting started

1. Set up two light zones (flower tent + veg space)
2. Start 4 plants from seed, 2 weeks apart
3. Take clones from the best performer during veg
4. Flip the oldest two to flower when ready
5. As each finishes, move the next veg plant into flower
6. Root new clones to replace them

Within 3–4 months, you'll be in rhythm — harvesting every 2–4 weeks,
never waiting, never running out. That's the perpetual promise.
