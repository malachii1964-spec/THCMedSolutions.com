A seedling is running on stored energy and a root system the
size of a hair. Your job for two weeks is simple: don't do
too much.

## The numbers that matter

| Factor | Target | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Light cycle | 18/6 | Vigorous growth without wasted power |
| Light distance | 45–60 cm (LED at ~30%) | Stretch means too far; bleach spots mean too close |
| Temperature | 22–26 °C day, 18–21 °C night | Cold shocks roots; heat dries seedlings out |
| Humidity | 65–70% RH | Seedlings drink through leaves while roots develop |
| Water pH | 6.0–6.5 (soil) | Nutrient availability window |

## Watering: the part everyone gets wrong

Overwatering kills more seedlings than everything else
combined. Roots need oxygen; saturated soil has none.

- Water in a **small ring around the stem**, not a flood —
  30–60 ml is plenty at first
- Let the top 2 cm of medium **dry out between waterings**
  (poke a finger in; if it's damp, wait)
- A light pot is a thirsty pot — lift it and learn the weight
  difference
- Drooping with **wet soil = overwatered**. Drooping with dry
  soil = thirsty. Check before you pour

The ring-watering technique forces roots to reach outward
toward the moisture, building a stronger root system. As the
plant grows, gradually widen the ring and increase the volume.
By the end of seedling stage, you should be wetting most of
the pot.

For a deeper dive on overwatering vs underwatering, see the
[watering diagnosis guide](/guides/overwatering-underwatering).

## Humidity: why seedlings need it high

Seedlings have almost no root system, so they absorb most of
their water through their leaves via transpiration. Low
humidity (below 50%) forces the tiny roots to work harder than
they can, leading to wilting even in moist soil.

**How to raise humidity cheaply:**
- A clear dome (humidity dome, cut water bottle, or clear
  plastic bag over the pot) traps moisture around the leaves
- Remove the dome for 15 minutes twice a day to exchange air
  and prevent damping off
- A small humidifier in the tent works; aim for 65–70%
- Group seedlings together — they humidify each other

Drop humidity gradually as roots develop: 65–70% in week 1,
55–65% by week 2, then down to standard veg range (50–60%)
once the plant is growing real fan leaves.

## Feeding: don't

Seed leaves (cotyledons) carry the nutrients a seedling needs
for the first 10–14 days, and most starting mixes carry more.
Feeding now burns roots.

Start at **quarter-strength** vegetative nutrients only after
the third set of true leaves, and only if the plant is pale.
If leaves are green and happy, the medium is still feeding
the plant — wait.

When you do start feeding, use a gentle seedling-strength
nutrient at pH 6.0–6.5 (soil) or 5.5–6.0 (coco). See the
[pH guide](/guides/ph-for-beginners) for calibration basics.

## Stretch and how to stop it

A seedling reaching for light gets tall, thin, and floppy.
If yours stretches:

1. Bring the light closer in small steps (watch the leaves
   for 24 h after each move)
2. Add a gentle fan — light air movement thickens stems
3. When transplanting, you can bury a stretched stem up to
   the first leaves

If the seedling has already fallen over, prop it up with a
toothpick or small stake and fix the light distance. It will
recover.

## What healthy seedlings look like

- **Color:** bright, even green. Dark green means the medium
  is hot (too much nutrient); yellowing from the bottom means
  the medium has run out
- **Growth rate:** one new set of leaves every 3–5 days. Slower
  than that usually means overwatering or cold temps
- **Stem:** short, sturdy, and slightly purple is fine (some
  genetics). Tall and white means stretching
- **Cotyledons:** the round seed leaves yellow and drop around
  day 10–14. This is normal — the plant has used their stored
  energy

## Common seedling problems

**Damping off** — the stem pinches at the soil line and the
seedling topples. Caused by too-wet conditions and poor
airflow. Prevention: don't overwater, use clean media, and
keep air moving. There's no fix once it happens — start a
new seed.

**Helmet head** — the seed shell stays stuck on the cotyledons.
Wait 24 hours; if it hasn't dropped, mist it and gently
remove it with tweezers. Don't force it — tearing the
cotyledons is worse than waiting.

**Curled or cupped leaves** — usually heat stress or light too
close. Raise the light 5 cm and check your canopy temperature.

For a full troubleshooting reference, see the
[seedling troubleshooting guide](/guides/seedling-problems-fixes).

## When seedling stage ends

You're in vegetative growth once the plant is producing
full-sized fan leaves at a visible weekly pace — usually
**week 2–3**. That's when feeding, training, and real light
intensity begin. See [vegetative stage 101](/guides/vegetative-stage-101)
for what comes next.
