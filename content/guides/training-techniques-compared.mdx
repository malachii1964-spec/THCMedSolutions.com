Training is the fastest path to more yield without spending more money.
But with half a dozen techniques, each with its own acronym and Reddit
following, it's hard to know which ones matter for your setup.

This guide puts them all in one place: what they do, when to use them,
how they combine, and which ones to skip as a beginner.

## The comparison table

| Technique | Yield boost | Difficulty | Recovery time | Best for |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|--------------|----------|
| LST (Low Stress Training) | +15–30% | Easy | None (no stress) | Every grow, every skill level |
| Topping | +20–40% | Easy | 3–7 days | Any grower after their first grow |
| FIM | +15–30% | Easy | 3–5 days | Same as topping with less precision needed |
| Mainlining / manifolding | +30–50% | Medium | 2–4 weeks total | Growers with time to veg longer |
| SCROG (Screen of Green) | +30–50% | Medium | None (passive) | Indoor tents with 1–4 plants |
| SOG (Sea of Green) | +20–30% | Easy | None | Many small plants, fast cycles |
| Supercropping | +10–20% | Medium | 3–7 days | Emergency height control, selective bending |
| Defoliation | +5–20% | Easy–Medium | 1–3 days | Dense canopies in mid-late flower |
| Lollipopping | +5–15% | Easy | None | Any indoor grow before flower |

## LST — Low Stress Training

**What it does:** bends branches down and ties them to the pot edge or
stakes, creating a flat, even canopy without cutting the plant.

**How to do it:**
1. Wait until the plant has 4–5 nodes
2. Gently bend the main stem 90° and tie it to the pot rim (use soft
   plant wire or garden clips, never string that cuts into stems)
3. As branches grow upward from the bent stem, tie those down too
4. Continue until the canopy is a flat, bush-like shape

**Why it works:** by bending the main stem below the side branches, you
break apical dominance. The plant distributes growth hormones (auxins)
more evenly, and every branch becomes a potential cola instead of just
the top.

**Combine with:** everything. LST is compatible with every other technique
and should be part of every indoor grow. It costs nothing and adds 15–30%
yield for 5 minutes of work per day.

## Topping

**What it does:** cuts the main growth tip, causing two branches to grow
from the cut site. Each new branch can be topped again, doubling your
cola count with each cut.

**How to do it:**
1. Wait until the plant has 5–6 nodes (3–4 weeks from seed)
2. Cut the stem cleanly above the 3rd or 4th node with sterilized
   scissors
3. Two branches grow from the cut site
4. Optionally top again after 1–2 weeks for 4 main colas

**Recovery:** 3–7 days of slowed growth while the plant redirects energy.
In that time, the side branches below the cut catch up.

**Don't top:**
- Autoflowers (no time to recover — top only if you're experienced)
- Seedlings (too young, too fragile)
- In flower (the plant can't recover)

See our [topping, FIM & mainlining guide](/guides/topping-fim-mainlining)
for detailed technique.

## FIM (F*** I Missed)

**What it does:** like topping, but you cut through the top growth tip
instead of removing it completely. This can produce 3–8 new tops instead
of 2, though results are less predictable.

**How it differs from topping:** topping gives you 2 clean tops every
time. FIMming gives you 3–8 tops but is messier — some of the new growth
may be asymmetric or stunted. Most growers prefer topping for its
consistency.

**When to FIM instead of top:** if you want more tops without multiple
rounds of topping. One FIM can achieve what two rounds of topping does,
saving a week of veg time.

## Mainlining / Manifolding

**What it does:** creates a perfectly symmetric plant by topping to 2 or
3 nodes and building an even "manifold" of branches from the base. The
result is 8 or 16 identical colas fed by equal-sized branches.

**The process:**
1. Top to the 3rd node when the plant has 5–6 nodes
2. Remove everything below the 3rd node (only 2 branches remain)
3. Let those 2 branches grow, then top each again → 4 branches
4. Top once more → 8 branches (or stop at 4 if space is limited)
5. Train all branches flat with LST

**Time cost:** mainlining adds 2–4 weeks of veg time compared to just
topping. The plant needs time to build the manifold structure.

**Is it worth it?** For 1–2 large plants in a 4×4, absolutely — the yield
per plant is substantially higher and the canopy is perfectly even. For
small pots, tight spaces, or quick cycles, it's overkill.

## SCROG — Screen of Green

**What it does:** a horizontal net (trellis) placed above the plants.
As branches grow through the net, you tuck them back under, spreading
the canopy horizontally until every square inch of net has a branch tip.

**The result:** a perfectly flat canopy where every bud site receives
equal light. This is the highest-yielding single-technique approach for
indoor tents.

**How to do it:**
1. Place a trellis net 8–12 inches above the pots (adjustable is better)
2. As branches grow through, tuck them back under toward empty squares
3. Continue tucking for 1–2 weeks into flower (during the stretch)
4. Stop tucking once buds start forming — let them grow vertically
   through the net

**Combine with:** topping (2–4 tops per plant, then SCROG to fill the
net). LST does similar things without a physical net, but SCROG gives
you a rigid, consistent framework.

**The catch:** SCROG makes it harder to move or inspect individual
plants. Once the net is woven, the plant is locked in place. Not ideal
if you need to rotate plants or pull one out for inspection.

See our [SCROG guide](/guides/scrog-screen-of-green) for the full
technique.

## SOG — Sea of Green

**What it does:** grows many small plants (from clones) at high density,
with no training. Each plant produces one main cola. The canopy fills
quickly because there are so many plants.

**The method:**
1. Take clones from a proven mother
2. Root them, then flip to flower after minimal veg (0–2 weeks)
3. Pack plants tight (1 per square foot)
4. Each plant produces 1 main cola (14–28g typically)
5. Harvest, repeat

**Why it works:** speed. A SOG cycle can harvest every 8–10 weeks
because there's almost no veg phase. The yield per plant is low, but
the yield per square foot per year is competitive.

**Legal note:** SOG requires more plants. If your jurisdiction has plant
limits, this may not be practical. Check your local laws.

## Supercropping

**What it does:** intentionally breaks the inner fibers of a branch
(without snapping it) by pinching and bending. The branch drops to
horizontal, then the broken point heals into a thick knuckle that
delivers more nutrients to the branch tip.

**When to use it:**
- Emergency height control (a branch is about to hit the light)
- Selective bending when LST isn't enough
- Strengthening branches that will support heavy buds

**How to do it:**
1. Squeeze the stem between your fingers at the point you want to bend
2. Roll and crush gently until the inner fibers give way (you'll feel
   it soften)
3. Bend the softened section 90°
4. Support the bent branch with tape or a stake if needed

**Recovery:** 3–7 days. The knuckle that forms is actually a feature —
it increases the stem's nutrient transport capacity.

## The combination matrix

Most growers use 2–3 techniques together:

| Setup | Recommended combination |
|-------|----------------------|
| First grow, 1–2 plants | LST only |
| Second grow, 2–4 plants | Topping + LST |
| 4×4 tent, 2 plants, max yield | Mainlining + SCROG |
| 4×4 tent, 4 plants, balanced | Topping + LST + light defoliation |
| Quick cycle, clone room | SOG (no training) |
| Tall sativa indoors | Topping + supercropping + SCROG |

## Common mistakes

1. **Training too early** — wait until the plant has at least 4–5 nodes
   and is growing vigorously. Training a struggling plant makes it worse
2. **Training in flower** — stop all high-stress training (topping,
   mainlining, supercropping) before the flip. LST and tucking are fine
   during the stretch
3. **Too many techniques at once** — pick 2–3 and get good at them.
   Topping + LST covers 90% of home growers
4. **Snapping branches** — if you snap a branch while supercropping,
   tape it immediately with plant tape or electrical tape. It will heal
   if the outer bark is intact
5. **Training autoflowers too aggressively** — autos have a fixed
   timeline. Heavy topping or mainlining steals weeks they don't have.
   LST is the safest training for autos

## The 80/20 recommendation

If you learn only two techniques:

1. **Topping** — double your cola count with one cut
2. **LST** — even out the canopy for free

Together, these deliver 80% of the yield improvement you'd get from any
combination. Everything else is optimization on top of a solid foundation.
