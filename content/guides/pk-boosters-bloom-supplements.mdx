PK boosters — supplements heavy in phosphorus (P) and potassium
(K) — are the most aggressively marketed products in the cannabis
nutrient industry. The promise: bigger, denser, heavier buds. The
reality is more nuanced. PK boosters can increase yield when used
correctly, but overuse is one of the most common causes of late-
flower nutrient problems.

## What PK boosters do

During flower, cannabis shifts its nutrient demand. Vegetative
growth consumed mostly nitrogen. Flower formation demands more
phosphorus (for energy transfer, flower initiation, and resin
production) and potassium (for enzyme activation, water regulation,
and bud density).

Base bloom nutrients already contain elevated P and K compared to
veg formulas. PK boosters add extra P and K on top of your base
nutrients during the peak flowering weeks when demand is highest.

### The biology

- **Phosphorus** drives ATP (energy) production, which fuels flower
  development, root growth, and trichome formation. Phosphorus demand
  peaks during weeks 3–6 of flower
- **Potassium** regulates water movement in cells, activates dozens
  of enzymes, and is critical for dense bud formation. Potassium
  demand increases throughout flower and peaks during weeks 5–7

## When to use PK boosters

### The window: weeks 3–6

Most PK boosters are designed for mid-flower:

| Week of flower | PK booster use |
|---------------|---------------|
| 1–2 (stretch) | No — still transitioning nutrients |
| 3–4 (bud formation) | Light dose (50% of label) |
| 4–5 (bud swell) | Full dose (peak P and K demand) |
| 5–6 (fattening) | Full dose or beginning to taper |
| 7+ (ripening) | Taper off; most growers stop PK boosters |
| Final 1–2 weeks | No PK booster; plain water or reduced feed |

### The 2-week sweet spot

Weeks 4–5 of flower are the highest-leverage weeks for PK
supplementation. This is when calyx production is at maximum rate
and the buds are actively building mass. Before week 3, the buds
aren't developed enough to use extra PK. After week 6, the plant
is ripening and slowing nutrient uptake.

### Which to buy

For most home growers, the base bloom nutrients provide adequate P
and K through flower. A PK booster is optional and provides
marginal improvement (5–15% yield increase when used correctly).

If you do use one:
- **Canna PK 13/14** is straightforward and hard to over-apply
- **GH Liquid KoolBloom** is widely available and well-documented
- **MKP** is the cheapest option (bulk chemical) but requires precise
  measurement — overdosing is easy and destructive

## How to dose PK boosters

### The golden rule: less is more

PK boosters are the most over-applied supplements in cannabis
growing. The nutrient companies set their recommended rates high
(they sell more product that way). Start at 50% of label and
increase only if the plant responds well.

### Dosing approach

1. **Start at 50% of the manufacturer's dose** during week 3
2. **Watch the plant** for 3–4 days. If no stress signs (leaf-tip
   burn, browning edges), maintain or increase slightly
3. **Peak at 75–100% of label** during weeks 4–5
4. **Taper:** reduce to 50%, then stop by week 6–7
5. **Monitor EC:** adding PK boosters on top of base nutrients
   increases your feed EC. Don't let total EC exceed 2.2 in most
   situations

### What overdose looks like

Excessive phosphorus and potassium cause problems that look
different from typical overfeeding:

**Phosphorus excess:**
- Locks out zinc, iron, and calcium
- Lower leaves develop rust-colored spots or necrosis (not the P
  deficiency you expected to fix)
- Stunted new growth from zinc lockout
- Burnt leaf tips progressing into leaf margins

**Potassium excess:**
- Locks out calcium and magnesium
- Brown, crispy leaf edges (looks like potassium deficiency
  ironically — but the cause is excess K locking out Ca and Mg)
- Interveinal chlorosis from magnesium lockout
- Salt buildup in the medium

**The irony:** many growers see the symptoms of PK overdose, think
they have a deficiency (because the lockout symptoms mimic
deficiency), and add MORE PK booster. This makes it worse.

### Recovery from PK overdose

1. **Stop the PK booster immediately**
2. **Flush the medium** with plain, pH'd water at 2× pot volume
3. **Resume with base bloom nutrients only** — no PK booster — for
   1–2 waterings
4. **Reintroduce PK booster at 25% dose** if needed, or skip it
   entirely for the rest of the grow

## The case against PK boosters

Some experienced growers skip PK boosters entirely and get
excellent results. Their argument:

- **Base bloom nutrients already contain elevated PK.** If you're
  using a cannabis-specific nutrient line at the recommended
  concentration, the P and K are already there
- **The yield increase is marginal (5–15%)** and only when timed
  perfectly. The risk of overdose and lockout may negate the benefit
- **Simple feeding programs produce more consistent results** than
  complex supplement stacks. Every additional product increases the
  chance of something going wrong
- **The "bigger buds" in marketing photos** are primarily the result
  of genetics, light intensity, and environment — not PK boosters

This is a valid position. If your base nutrients produce healthy
plants and dense buds, a PK booster is optimization, not necessity.

## Bloom supplements beyond PK

### Sugars and carbohydrates (molasses, Sweet, Bud Candy)

**The claim:** feed the roots and soil microbes with sugars to
enhance flavor and density.

**The reality:** in living soil, blackstrap molasses (1 tbsp per
gallon of water) feeds beneficial microbes and is cheap and
effective. Commercial sugar supplements (Advanced Nutrients Bud
Candy, Botanicare Sweet) do the same thing at 10× the price.

In hydro or sterile media: sugars feed bacteria, not just beneficial
ones. Adding sugars to a DWC reservoir promotes harmful bacterial
growth. Don't.

### Amino acid supplements

**The claim:** provide building blocks for protein synthesis and
terpene production.

**The reality:** some evidence supports amino acid supplementation
improving terpene profiles and stress resistance. Products like
Advanced Nutrients B-52 and Canna Bio Boost contain amino acids.
The effect is subtle and difficult to verify at home.

### Humic and fulvic acids

**The claim:** improve nutrient availability and root health.

**The reality:** humic and fulvic acids genuinely improve nutrient
chelation and availability in soil and coco. They're most useful
in organic grows. Products like RAW Humic Acid or Diamond Nectar
are worth considering, especially if you're in soil with organic
amendments.

### The supplement stack reality

Most home growers will see their biggest improvements from dialing
in three things: **light intensity, environment (VPD), and base
nutrients.** These account for 90%+ of yield and quality. Supplements
are the final 5–10% — nice to have, but never a substitute for
getting the basics right.

## Common mistakes

1. **Starting PK boosters too early** — weeks 1–2 of flower is still
   transition. The plant needs nitrogen for the stretch, not extra
   PK. Wait until week 3
2. **Dosing at full label strength from day one** — nutrient
   companies recommend aggressive doses. Start at 50%
3. **Stacking multiple bloom supplements** — PK booster + bloom
   enhancer + carb load + bud hardener = salt overload. Pick one PK
   booster and skip the rest, or use base nutrients alone
4. **Not monitoring EC when adding boosters** — PK boosters raise
   your total EC. If base nutrients are at EC 1.6 and the PK booster
   adds 0.4, you're at 2.0 — check this, and adjust base nutrients
   down if needed
5. **Continuing PK boosters into the final week** — the plant is
   senescing (shutting down). Extra nutrients at this point don't
   increase yield — they increase residual mineral content in the
   flower
