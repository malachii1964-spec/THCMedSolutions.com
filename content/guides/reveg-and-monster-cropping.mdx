Most cannabis plants live one life: sprout, veg, flower, harvest,
compost. But cannabis is a photoperiod-responsive annual — not a
genetically programmed annual. It flowers because the light cycle
tells it to, not because it's reached the end of its biological
lifespan. Switch the light back to 18/6 after harvest, and the plant
will slowly, weirdly, revert to vegetative growth.

This is re-vegging. It's slow, it's strange-looking, and it's not for
every situation — but it lets you preserve genetics without taking
clones, harvest twice from one root system, and discover phenos
you missed.

Monster cropping is the related technique of taking clones from a
flowering plant. The clones revert to veg and develop an unusually
bushy structure that's ideal for training.

## Re-vegging: the second harvest

### When to re-veg

- **You found a special phenotype** and didn't take clones in veg
  (the most common reason). Re-vegging is your only option to keep it
- **You want a second harvest** from the same root system without
  starting from seed
- **You want to preserve genetics** that you can't replace (rare
  seeds, discontinued strains)

### When NOT to re-veg

- **You have clones or seeds.** Starting a new plant is faster and
  produces healthier growth. Re-vegging takes 4–8 weeks before the
  plant looks normal again
- **Autoflowers.** Autoflowers can't re-veg — they flower based on
  age, not light cycle. Once they're done, they're done
- **Stressed or unhealthy plants.** Re-vegging requires the plant to
  have enough energy reserves to restart growth. A plant that barely
  survived flower won't survive re-veg

### How to re-veg

**Step 1: Harvest selectively**

Don't harvest the entire plant. Leave the bottom 1/3 with small buds
and fan leaves intact. These remaining leaves provide the
photosynthetic capacity the plant needs to restart vegetative growth.

Removing the top 2/3 of buds is your harvest. The bottom 1/3 is the
plant's recovery reserve.

**Step 2: Switch the light cycle**

Immediately move the plant back to 18/6 (or 20/4). The extended light
period triggers the hormonal shift from flowering back to vegetative
growth.

**Step 3: Feed vegetative nutrients**

Switch back to a nitrogen-heavy veg formula. The plant needs nitrogen
for the new leaf growth it's about to produce. Start at half strength
— the reduced canopy can't handle full-strength feeds.

**Step 4: Wait (the ugly phase)**

For the first 2–4 weeks, the plant looks terrible:
- Remaining buds develop strange, single-bladed leaves emerging from
  the calyxes (this is the classic "re-veg" look)
- Growth is slow and confused-looking
- New shoots appear from the main stem, often with abnormal leaf
  shapes (single or triple blades instead of the normal 5–7)

This is completely normal. The plant is hormonally confused — it's
reversing a deeply committed developmental program. The weird growth
is the transition.

**Step 5: Clean up and train**

Once new vegetative growth is clearly established (normal-looking
leaves with 5+ blades, 4–6 weeks in):
- Remove all remaining bud material — it's dead weight now
- Remove the weird single-blade transition growth
- Begin LST or topping the new vegetative branches
- Increase nutrients to full veg strength

**Step 6: Flower again**

When the re-vegged plant has filled out (typically 4–8 weeks of veg
after reveg begins), flip to 12/12 for the second flower cycle.

### What to expect from a re-veg harvest

- **Yield:** usually 60–80% of the first harvest. The root system is
  mature and large (an advantage), but the plant's overall vigor is
  reduced compared to a fresh start
- **Quality:** similar to the first harvest if the genetics are
  stable. Some growers report slightly different terpene profiles
  on the second run
- **Structure:** re-vegged plants are often bushier than the original
  because multiple new shoots emerge from every old node. This can be
  an advantage for training

### Timeline

| Phase | Duration | What's happening |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| Selective harvest | Day 0 | Remove top 2/3, keep bottom 1/3 |
| Ugly phase | Weeks 1–4 | Single-blade leaves, slow weird growth |
| Transition | Weeks 4–6 | Normal leaves return, plant looks healthy |
| Veg growth | Weeks 6–10 | Train and fill out the canopy |
| Flower | Weeks 10–18+ | Second flower cycle |
| **Total second cycle** | **18–22 weeks** | vs. 12–16 weeks from seed/clone |

The total time is longer than starting fresh. Re-vegging is a
preservation technique, not a speed technique.

## Monster cropping: clones from flower

### What it is

Monster cropping means taking cuttings from a plant that's already
in flower (typically weeks 2–3 of 12/12). When these cuttings are
placed under 18/6 light, they revert to vegetative growth — and the
reversal produces an extremely bushy plant with heavy branching and
no apical dominance.

The name comes from the result: a "monster" plant that branches so
aggressively it can fill a SCROG net in half the time of a normal
clone.

### Why monster cropping works

When a flowering clone reverts to veg:
- Every node produces multiple shoots instead of one (the reversal
  breaks normal apical dominance)
- The internodal spacing is short and tight
- The plant bushes out naturally without topping or training

This growth pattern is ideal for SOG (Sea of Green) and SCROG
setups. The plant fills horizontal space quickly without the weeks
of training that a regular clone or seedling requires.

### How to monster crop

**Step 1: Select the cutting**

Choose a healthy branch from a plant in weeks 2–3 of flower:
- 6–8 inches long
- Has several nodes
- From the lower/middle portion of the plant (not the main cola)
- Has small bud sites forming (these will be absorbed during
  reversion)

**Step 2: Take the cutting**

1. Cut at a 45° angle just below a node
2. Immediately place the cut end in water or cloning solution
3. Remove the lowest 1–2 sets of leaves/bud sites
4. Trim remaining fan leaves by 50% (reduce transpiration demand
   while roots develop)

**Step 3: Root the clone**

Root the cutting exactly as you would a normal clone:
- Dip in rooting hormone (Clonex gel)
- Place in a Rapid Rooter, rockwool cube, or aeroponic cloner
- Maintain high humidity (dome or humidity tent)
- Light: 18/6, low intensity (CFL or dimmed LED)

**Rooting time:** flowering clones typically take longer to root
than veg clones — 10–21 days vs. 7–14. Be patient. The plant is
simultaneously rooting AND beginning the hormonal shift from
flower to veg.

**Step 4: The reversion**

Once rooted, the clone will display the same "ugly phase" as a
re-vegged plant:
- Single-blade leaves emerging from bud sites
- Slow, strange-looking growth
- Gradually transitioning to normal vegetative leaves

This phase lasts 2–4 weeks. During this time, keep the light on
18/6 and feed very light veg nutrients.

**Step 5: The explosion**

Once normal leaves appear, the monster-cropped clone will begin
branching aggressively. Multiple shoots emerge from every node.
The plant bushes out horizontally without intervention.

At this point:
- Increase light intensity to normal veg levels
- Feed full-strength veg nutrients
- Begin training if desired (SCROG is ideal)
- The plant will fill a SCROG net much faster than a normal clone

### Success rate

Monster cropping has a lower success rate than standard cloning:
- Standard veg clones: 90–95% success with proper technique
- Monster crops: 50–70% success. Many cuttings fail to root or fail
  to revert cleanly

**Tip:** take 2–3× more cuttings than you need. If you want 4
monster-cropped plants, take 8–12 cuttings. Select the best rooters.

### Which strains monster crop well

- **Indica-dominant hybrids** generally revert faster and more
  cleanly than pure sativas
- Strains known for cloning vigor: OG Kush, GSC, Blue Dream,
  Gelato, Wedding Cake
- Autoflowers cannot be monster cropped (they can't revert to veg)

## Comparing the techniques

| Factor | Re-veg | Monster crop |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Purpose | Second harvest / preserve genetics | Bushy clones for SCROG/SOG |
| Source | Harvested plant | Flowering branch (clone) |
| Time to usable plant | 6–10 weeks | 4–8 weeks |
| Success rate | High (if plant is healthy) | 50–70% |
| Result | Bushier version of original | Extremely bushy, multi-branched |
| Applies to autos? | No | No |

## Common mistakes

1. **Harvesting too completely before re-veg** — leave the bottom 1/3
   with leaves. The plant needs photosynthetic capacity to restart.
   Stripping it bare and expecting recovery is asking too much
2. **Taking monster crop cuttings too late** — cuttings from week 5+
   of flower have heavily developed buds that are difficult to
   revert. Week 2–3 is the sweet spot
3. **Impatience during the ugly phase** — the weird single-blade
   growth after reversion is normal and temporary. Don't throw the
   plant out. Wait 4 weeks before judging
4. **Feeding flower nutrients during re-veg** — the plant needs
   nitrogen for new vegetative growth. Switch immediately to veg
   formula
5. **Expecting normal timelines** — both techniques add weeks to the
   cycle. If speed matters, start a new plant from seed or clone.
   Re-veg and monster cropping are for preservation and structure,
   not efficiency
