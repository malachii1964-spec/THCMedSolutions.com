Cannabis odor is the most common reason indoor grows get
discovered by neighbors, landlords, or roommates. A single
flowering plant in weeks 5–8 produces enough volatile terpenes to
permeate walls, doors, and HVAC ducts. "I'll just crack a window"
doesn't work. Neither does air freshener — it mixes with the
cannabis smell and produces something worse.

If stealth matters to you, odor control needs to be engineered
into the grow from day one, not added as an afterthought when the
smell becomes a problem.

## When does cannabis smell?

### The smell timeline

| Stage | Smell level | Notes |
|-------|------------|-------|
| Germination | None | Seeds and seedlings have minimal odor |
| Seedling (weeks 1–2) | None to very faint | Handling leaves may release faint scent |
| Early veg (weeks 3–4) | Faint | Some strains emit mild odor when disturbed |
| Late veg (weeks 5–8) | Mild to moderate | Stems release scent when bent (LST) |
| Early flower (weeks 1–2) | Moderate | The smell begins — carbon filter should be running |
| Mid flower (weeks 3–5) | Strong | Peak terpene production begins. This is when it gets real |
| Late flower (weeks 6–8+) | Very strong to overwhelming | Maximum odor. Without filtration, it fills the house |
| Drying | Very strong | Fresh-harvested cannabis is at peak smell |
| Curing | Moderate (contained in jars) | Opening jars releases concentrated aroma |

**The critical period:** weeks 3–8 of flower. This is when
terpene production is at maximum and the plant is continuously
releasing volatile aromatic compounds. Your carbon filter must be
running well before this period starts.

### Which strains smell the most?

All cannabis smells. But some strains are notably more pungent:

**Extremely loud:** Cheese, Sour Diesel, Gorilla Glue, Skunk,
AK-47, Super Skunk, White Truffle

**Moderately loud:** Northern Lights (relatively low for its
quality), Blue Dream, Girl Scout Cookies

**Relatively quiet:** most autoflowers (shorter flower = shorter
peak smell), some CBD strains

"Low-odor" strains still smell strongly during flower. The
difference is relative — a "quiet" strain fills a room, while a
"loud" strain fills a house.

## Odor control methods

### 1. Carbon filter + inline fan (the standard)

**How it works:** air passes through a bed of activated carbon.
The carbon adsorbs odor molecules (terpenes, volatile organic
compounds) from the air. The exhaust is essentially odor-free.

**Effectiveness:** 95–99% odor removal when properly installed
and maintained.

**Setup:**
- Carbon filter is connected directly to the inline fan inside
  the tent
- Air path: tent air → through carbon filter → through fan →
  out ducting → outside tent
- The filter must be sized to match the fan's CFM rating

**Maintenance:**
- Replace every 12–24 months (or when you start smelling odor
  from the exhaust)
- Wash/replace the prefilter sock every 2–3 months
- Humidity above 70% degrades carbon faster — keep flower humidity
  below 55% for maximum filter life

sizing details.

### 2. Negative pressure (essential)

The carbon filter is useless if odor escapes through gaps and
zippers instead of through the filter. Negative pressure ensures
ALL air exits through the filter:

- Exhaust fan pulls air out faster than it enters
- Tent walls should pull inward slightly when zipped
- Any air leak is INWARD (clean air in), not outward (smelly
  air out)

**Check for leaks:** with the exhaust running and tent zipped,
walk around the tent and sniff at every seam, zipper, and port.
If you smell cannabis, there's a positive pressure leak. Seal it
or increase exhaust speed.

### 3. Ducting the exhaust

Where you duct the exhaust matters:

**Best:** out a window or through an exterior wall (smell goes
outside and disperses)

**Good:** into another room or attic space (dilutes before
reaching living areas)

**Acceptable:** into the same room as the tent (carbon filter
removes odor, but any filter failure is immediately noticeable)

**Bad:** into HVAC return ducts or central air system — this
pushes grow room air to every room in the house through the
existing ductwork

### 4. Odor neutralizer gels

Products like ONA (Odor Neutralizer Agent) or Cannabolish gel
use essential oils that chemically neutralize terpene molecules
in the air.

**How to use:**
- Place outside the tent (never inside — it can affect terpene
  development on buds)
- Position near the tent exhaust outlet, doorways, or in
  hallways between the grow space and living areas
- Works as a secondary layer alongside carbon filtration

**Limitations:**
- Won't handle the volume of odor from an unfiltered grow
- Some products have their own strong scent
- Needs replacement every 4–6 weeks

### 5. Ozone generators (use with extreme caution)

Ozone (O3) oxidizes odor molecules, eliminating them. Some
growers use inline ozone generators in the exhaust ducting as
a secondary treatment after the carbon filter.

**Why we don't recommend them:**
- Ozone is toxic to humans at concentrations effective for odor
  control
- Ozone damages plant tissue (never use inside the grow space)
- Prolonged exposure degrades rubber, plastic, and electrical
  insulation
- In a residential setting, ozone leaks pose health risks to
  everyone in the house
- Carbon filters alone are sufficient for 99% of home grows

If you insist on ozone, use only in-duct generators with a timer,
never in living spaces, and never inside the grow tent.

### 6. Air purifiers (supplemental)

HEPA air purifiers in the room outside the tent can catch residual
odor molecules that slip past the carbon filter. They're not a
primary solution but help as a final polishing layer.

Look for units with activated carbon pre-filters — the carbon
component does the odor work, not the HEPA (which captures
particles, not gases).

## Smell management during drying

Drying is the most overlooked odor challenge. Fresh-harvested
cannabis at peak terpene content, hung in an open room, produces
MORE smell than a flowering tent with a carbon filter running.

**Solutions:**
- **Dry inside the tent** with the carbon filter running. Turn
  off the grow light, leave the exhaust on low. The tent is
  already set up for odor control
- **Dedicated drying space** with its own carbon filter and fan
  if the tent is needed for the next cycle
- **Drying boxes/cabinets** with small carbon filters for single-
  plant harvests
- **Never dry in an open room** in a shared space — the smell
  will permeate everything

See our [drying guide](/guides/drying-room-setup).

## Smell management during trimming

Trimming produces intense, concentrated bursts of terpene odor:
- Trim inside the tent (with filter running) or near an exhaust
  vent
- Wear gloves — resin on your hands transfers the smell to
  everything you touch
- Bag trim waste immediately in sealed bags
- ONA gel or Cannabolish near the trimming area helps

## Apartment-specific strategies

Growing in an apartment adds layers of concern: shared HVAC,
thin walls, common hallways, landlord inspections.

1. **Seal the grow room door** — weatherstripping and a door
   sweep prevent smell from entering hallways
2. **Don't exhaust into the hallway** — duct to a window if
   possible. If not, ensure the carbon filter is functioning
   perfectly and exhausting into the apartment (not common areas)
3. **Time-sensitive activities:** trim, dry, and open jars when
   neighbors are less likely to notice (late evening, early
   morning)
4. **Consider a small grow** — a 2×2 tent with 1–2 small plants
   produces much less odor than a 4×4 with 4 large plants. Less
   smell = less risk of detection
5. **Know your rights** — in states where home growing is legal,
   a landlord may still prohibit it in the lease. Check before
   investing in equipment

## Common mistakes

1. **Waiting until flower to install the carbon filter** — install
   and test your filter during setup, before germinating. If it
   fails during flower, you have an emergency
2. **Running the carbon filter without negative pressure** — the
   filter treats the air that passes through it. If air escapes
   through tent gaps instead, the filter is doing nothing for that
   portion of the smell
3. **Using an undersized carbon filter** — a filter rated below
   your fan's CFM can't treat all the air. Air rushes through too
   fast for the carbon to adsorb odor molecules. Match filter
   rating to fan CFM
4. **Opening the tent frequently during flower** — every time
   you unzip the tent, concentrated terpene-rich air pours out.
   Minimize openings. Do all maintenance in one session rather
   than opening the tent 5 times a day
5. **Ignoring smell from the drain** — if your tent drain line or
   runoff waste goes to a sink or drain, terpene-rich water can
   make the drain smell like cannabis. Flush with clean water
   after disposing of runoff
6. **Forgetting about the drying phase** — growers spend weeks
   with perfect odor control in flower, then hang plants in an
   open room to dry and the whole house smells for two weeks
