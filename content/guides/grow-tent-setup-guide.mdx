A grow tent is the most popular way to grow cannabis indoors. It's
cheaper than building a grow room, fully portable, and light-tight out of
the box. But setting one up properly is the difference between dialed
environment and a hot, humid disaster. This guide covers everything from
choosing a tent to running your first seed.

## Choosing a tent size

| Tent size | Plant count | Best for |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 2×2 ft (60×60 cm) | 1–2 plants | First grow, small space, veg/clone chamber |
| 2×4 ft (60×120 cm) | 2–4 plants | Most popular starter size, good balance |
| 4×4 ft (120×120 cm) | 4–6 plants | Serious home grow, room to train |
| 5×5 ft (150×150 cm) | 6–9 plants | Dedicated grower, excellent yield potential |
| 4×8 ft (120×240 cm) | 8–12 plants | Near-commercial, often divided veg/flower |

**Recommendation for first grow:** 2×4 or 4×4. The 2×4 is affordable and
fits in a closet; the 4×4 gives room to grow into the hobby.

**Height matters:** get at least 6 feet (180 cm) tall. You need space for
the light, the gap between light and canopy, and the plant itself. Short
tents create heat problems because there's no room between the light and
the exhaust fan.

## What goes inside the tent

### The essentials (buy these first)

1. **Grow light** — LED is the standard. For a 4×4, a 400–480W LED
   (Samsung LM301B/H diodes). For a 2×4, a 200–240W LED. See our
   [lighting guide](/guides/lighting-basics-ppfd) for PPFD targets.

2. **Inline exhaust fan + carbon filter** — pulls hot, humid, smelly air
   out. Size it for 1–2 full air exchanges per minute. A 4×4 tent is
   ~64 cubic feet; a 6" inline fan rated at 400+ CFM handles it easily
   with the carbon filter's drag.

3. **Clip fans (1–2)** — oscillating clip fans aimed below the canopy.
   Moving air prevents hot spots, strengthens stems, and discourages
   mold. Never aim directly at seedlings — gentle breeze, not a hurricane.

4. **Pots with saucers** — fabric pots (Smart Pots) in 3–5 gallon for
   flower. Solo cups or 1/2 gallon for seedlings/early veg.

5. **Growing medium** — for first grow, use a quality potting mix
   (Fox Farm Ocean Forest, Roots Organics, or coco coir + perlite).

6. **pH pen + EC/TDS meter** — non-negotiable. See our
   [watering guide](/guides/watering-ph-fundamentals).

7. **Timer** — for automating the light cycle. Mechanical timers work
   fine; smart plugs let you control remotely.

8. **Temperature/humidity monitor** — at minimum a cheap thermo-hygrometer.
   Better: a data-logging sensor like the Govee WiFi model.

### Nice to have (add later)

- **Trellis net** — for SCROG training
- **Ratchet hangers** — for adjustable light height
- **Drip trays or plant risers** — keeps pots off the tent floor
- **Intake fan or passive intake** — most tents work with passive intake
  (negative pressure pulls air in through bottom vents)
- **Humidifier or dehumidifier** — for dialing RH by stage

## Assembly: step by step

### 1. Choose the location

- **Near a window or vent** for ducting exhaust air outside
- **On a hard floor** if possible (carpet holds moisture and mold)
- **Away from exterior walls** in cold climates (temperature swings)
- **Near a power outlet** — grow tents pull 300–600W; don't daisy-chain
  power strips

### 2. Assemble the frame

Follow the manufacturer's instructions. Use all the corner connectors and
cross bars — they prevent the top from sagging under the weight of the
light and fan. Tighten everything fully.

### 3. Hang the light

Use ratchet hangers from the top cross bars. Hang the light at the
manufacturer's recommended height for seedlings (usually 24–30 inches
above the canopy at reduced power). You'll lower it as the plants grow.

**Check for light leaks** — zip the tent closed, turn on the light, and
look from outside with the room dark. Patch any pin-holes with tape.
Light leaks during the dark period can cause hermaphrodites.

### 4. Install the exhaust system

The standard configuration:

```
[Carbon filter inside tent, high]
        ↓ (ducting)
[Inline fan, at top or outside tent]
        ↓ (ducting)
[Vent outside tent → window/room]
```

- Mount the carbon filter high inside the tent (heat rises, odor rises)
- Connect ducting to the fan
- Route the exhaust outside the tent
- Use duct clamps at every connection — duct tape alone will fail

**Pro tip:** mount the fan outside the tent if possible. It reduces
noise inside and frees up ceiling space.

### 5. Set up clip fans

- One below the canopy, aimed across the pots (air circulation at soil
  level prevents fungus gnats and stagnant moisture)
- One above the canopy, oscillating (moves air around the leaves)
- Don't point fans directly at seedlings or clones

### 6. Place pots and medium

Set pots on plant risers or drip trays. Never let pots sit in standing
water — drain saucers after watering. If using fabric pots, they can go
directly on the tent floor, but risers improve air pruning and drainage.

### 7. Set the light timer

| Stage | Light schedule |
|-------|---------------|
| Seedling/Veg | 18 hours on / 6 hours off |
| Flower | 12 hours on / 12 hours off |
| Autoflower | 20 hours on / 4 hours off (entire life) |

Set the "on" period to align with when you're awake — easier to check on
plants, and the "dark period" happens at night when ambient temps are
cooler (which helps with heat management).

## Environment targets

| Parameter | Seedling | Veg | Early flower | Late flower |
|-----------|---------|-----|-------------|-------------|
| Temperature | 72–78°F | 72–82°F | 68–78°F | 65–75°F |
| Humidity (RH) | 65–70% | 55–65% | 45–55% | 40–50% |
| Light distance | 24–30" | 18–24" | 16–20" | 16–20" |

**The single biggest beginner mistake:** running the tent too hot. If
your tent is consistently above 85°F, add more exhaust power, dim the
light slightly, or run lights at night when ambient temps are lower.

## First-grow checklist

Before dropping your first seed:

- [ ] Tent assembled, all zippers work, no light leaks
- [ ] Light hung with ratchet hangers at seedling height
- [ ] Exhaust fan + carbon filter connected, running, pulling air out
- [ ] At least one clip fan circulating air inside
- [ ] Pots with medium, pre-moistened (not soaking wet)
- [ ] pH pen calibrated, EC meter ready
- [ ] Timer set to 18/6 (or 20/4 for autos)
- [ ] Thermo-hygrometer reading reasonable (72–78°F, 60–70% RH)
- [ ] Drainage plan (saucers, drip tray, or wet-vac for runoff)

If all boxes are checked, you're ready to germinate. See our
[germination guide](/guides/germinate-cannabis-seeds) for the next step.

## Budget breakdown

A complete 4×4 setup runs $400–800 depending on light quality:

| Item | Budget | Mid-range |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 4×4 tent | $70–100 | $100–150 |
| LED light (400W) | $100–200 | $200–350 |
| Inline fan + carbon filter | $70–120 | $100–160 |
| Clip fans (2) | $20–30 | $30–50 |
| Pots, medium, saucers | $30–50 | $40–60 |
| pH pen + EC meter | $20–40 | $40–80 |
| Timer, hangers, ducting | $20–40 | $30–50 |
| **Total** | **$330–580** | **$540–900** |

You don't need the most expensive gear to grow excellent cannabis. A
$150 light with good diodes (Samsung LM301B) outperforms a $400 blurple
panel from 2018. Spend smart, not big.
