The seedling stage is the most fragile period in a cannabis plant's
life. A seedling has a tiny root system, thin stems, and no energy
reserves — it relies entirely on the environment you provide. The
good news: seedlings need very little. The bad news: "very little"
is precise, and most seedling deaths come from too much of something,
not too little.

This guide covers everything from the moment the seed cracks until
the plant has 4–5 nodes and is ready for vegetative training.

## The first 48 hours

### What to expect

After planting a germinated seed (tap root visible) about 1/2 inch
deep in moist medium:

- **Hours 1–24:** nothing visible above the soil. The tap root is
  growing downward and anchoring
- **Hours 24–48:** the seedling breaks the surface. A pale, arched
  stem pushes up, carrying the seed shell
- **Hours 48–72:** the stem straightens and the cotyledons (round
  embryonic leaves) spread open. The seed shell may fall off on its
  own or remain attached

### If the seed shell is stuck

Don't panic. If the seed shell remains on the cotyledons after 48
hours above soil:
- **Mist the shell** with water to soften it
- **Wait another 24 hours** — most shells fall off naturally
- **If still stuck after 72 hours:** very gently remove it with
  tweezers. Hold the stem with one finger for support and peel the
  shell off carefully. Don't force it — if it resists, mist again
  and wait

### If the seedling is leggy (tall and thin)

A seedling stretching toward the light with a long, thin stem is
getting insufficient light. Fix immediately:
- Lower the light (or increase intensity)
- Bury the stem slightly by mounding soil around the base — cannabis
  stems can root from buried sections

## Environment: weeks 1–3

### Light

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Intensity (PPFD) | 200–350 µmol/m²/s |
| DLI | 15–25 mol/m²/day |
| Light height (LED) | 24–36 inches at 50% dimmer |
| Schedule (photoperiod) | 18/6 |
| Schedule (autoflower) | 20/4 |

Seedlings don't need much light. A 300W LED at 50% dimmer and 30
inches away is usually perfect. More light doesn't make seedlings
grow faster — it stresses them. Increase intensity gradually over
weeks 2–3. See our
[seedling lighting guide](/guides/seedling-lighting-schedule).

### Temperature

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Day (lights on) | 72–78°F (22–26°C) |
| Night (lights off) | 65–70°F (18–21°C) |
| Root zone | 72–76°F (22–24°C) |

**Cold root zones are the hidden killer.** If your pot is sitting on
a cold concrete floor, the root zone can be 10–15°F below ambient
air temperature. At 60°F root temp, seedling growth stalls. Use a
seedling heat mat set to 75°F under the pot.

### Humidity

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Relative humidity | 65–75% |
| Method | Humidity dome, humidifier, or covered tray |

Seedlings have tiny root systems that can't supply water fast
enough if the air is dry. High humidity reduces transpiration
(water loss through leaves), keeping the seedling hydrated even
with minimal roots.

**Humidity dome:** place a clear plastic dome (or a cut plastic
bottle) over the seedling for the first 5–7 days. Remove it once
the first true leaves are fully developed — the plant is now
transpiring enough to benefit from normal humidity.

### Airflow

Gentle airflow is important even for seedlings:
- A small fan on low, pointed near (not directly at) the seedling
- The slight movement strengthens the stem (mechanical stress
  stimulates thicker stem growth)
- Don't blast the seedling — a gentle breeze that makes the leaves
  barely sway is perfect

## Watering seedlings

### How much

Seedlings need very little water. The root system is a few inches
at most — water beyond the root zone sits unused and creates
anaerobic conditions.

**Method: the ring technique**
- Water in a small ring around the seedling, 2–3 inches from the
  stem
- Don't water directly on the stem (stem rot risk)
- Use 50–100 mL per watering for a seedling in a large pot
- As the plant grows, widen the ring to encourage roots to grow
  outward seeking water

### How often

- **Soil:** every 3–5 days for a seedling in a large pot. The small
  plant uses very little water. Stick your finger in the soil — if
  the top inch is dry, water lightly
- **Coco:** every 1–2 days, but very small amounts. Coco dries
  faster than soil
- **The biggest mistake:** overwatering. Seedlings die from drowning
  more than from any other cause. When in doubt, wait another day.
  See our
  [overwatering guide](/guides/overwatering-underwatering)

### Water quality

- **Plain water** for the first week (no nutrients)
- **pH adjusted:** 6.0–6.5 for soil, 5.5–6.0 for coco
- **Room temperature:** cold water shocks roots. Let water sit for
  an hour before using
- **Chlorinated tap water:** let it sit uncovered for 24 hours to
  off-gas chlorine, or use a dechlorinator

## When to start feeding

### In soil

If you're using amended soil (Fox Farms Happy Frog, Roots Organics):
- **Don't feed for 2–3 weeks.** The soil contains enough nutrients
  for a seedling
- **Hot soils (Ocean Forest):** may burn seedlings. Start in Happy
  Frog or a 50/50 Ocean Forest + perlite mix, then transplant into
  the hotter soil later
- **First feed:** at week 2–3, start with 25% of the manufacturer's
  recommended dose

### In coco

Coco has no nutrients. Feeding must start earlier:
- **Week 1:** very light feed — EC 0.3–0.4 (25% strength)
- **Week 2:** EC 0.5–0.8 (50% strength)
- **Week 3:** EC 0.8–1.0 (50–75% strength)
- **Cal-mag from the start** — coco always needs supplemental
  calcium and magnesium. See our
  [cal-mag guide](/guides/cal-mag-complete-guide)

### Signs of overfeeding a seedling

- Dark green leaves (too much nitrogen)
- Burnt leaf tips (nutrient burn)
- Curled or clawed leaf tips
- Slow or stunted growth despite good environment

If you see these signs, flush with plain pH'd water and skip the
next feeding.

## Growth milestones: weeks 1–3

| Day | What you should see |
|-----|-------------------|
| 1–3 | Cotyledons open, stem vertical |
| 4–7 | First pair of true leaves (single-blade, serrated) |
| 7–10 | Second pair of true leaves (3 blades) |
| 10–14 | Third pair (5 blades), visible stem thickening |
| 14–21 | 4th–5th node, roots reaching pot edges, accelerating growth |

**By day 21**, the seedling transitions to the vegetative stage.
Growth accelerates noticeably — you may see a new node every 1–2
days. The plant is ready for regular feeding, training, and normal
environmental conditions.

## Transplanting

If you started in a small container (solo cup, jiffy peat pot):
transplant when roots appear at the bottom drainage holes, usually
around day 10–14.

- **Water the seedling 1 hour before transplanting** — moist soil
  holds together; dry soil crumbles and exposes roots
- **Pre-moisten the new pot's medium** before transplanting
- **Handle by the soil, not the stem** — stem damage at this stage
  can be fatal
- **Plant at the same depth** — or slightly deeper to bury a leggy
  stem (cannabis can root from buried stem tissue)
- See our [transplanting guide](/guides/transplanting-cannabis)

## Common mistakes

1. **Overwatering** — the #1 seedling killer. A seedling in a
   5-gallon pot needs tablespoons of water, not cups. Water less than
   you think you should
2. **Too much light** — a 480W LED at 100% and 12 inches will
   bleach a seedling in hours. Start at 50% dimmer and 30+ inches.
   Increase gradually
3. **Hot soil** — Ocean Forest is too nitrogen-rich for seedlings.
   Start in a milder medium and transplant later
4. **No humidity dome** — dry air (40–50% RH) dehydrates seedlings
   faster than their tiny roots can supply water. Use a dome for the
   first week
5. **Starting nutrients immediately** — in soil, the medium feeds the
   seedling. In coco, start at 25% strength. Full-strength nutrients
   on a seedling cause nutrient burn
6. **Planting too deep** — the seed should be 1/2 inch deep, not 2
   inches. Deep planting means the seedling exhausts its energy
   pushing through soil before reaching light
