Harvest a week early and you lose potency you spent four months
building. The calendar and the breeder's "8 weeks" are estimates
— trichomes are the instrument panel.

## What you're looking at

Trichomes are the resin glands frosting your buds — tiny
mushroom-shaped stalks where cannabinoids and terpenes are
made. Their color tracks ripeness:

| Trichome state | What it means | Effect profile |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Clear, glassy | Still producing — too early | Weak, racy, headache-prone |
| Cloudy / milky | Peak THC | Potent, energetic-leaning |
| Amber | THC degrading to CBN | Heavier, more sedative |

The transition from clear to cloudy happens over 1–2 weeks.
From cloudy to amber takes another 1–2 weeks. Your total
harvest window is about **10–14 days** once trichomes start
turning milky.

## The tool

A **60–100x jeweler's loupe** or a cheap USB microscope.
Your phone camera at max zoom works in a pinch but flattens
the detail that matters.

**Where to look matters:** check the trichomes **on the buds**,
not the sugar leaves — leaf trichomes mature faster and will
mislead you early.

Check three spots: a top cola, a middle bud, and a lower bud.
They ripen in that order. If the top cola is mostly amber
but lower buds are still mostly milky, consider a staged
harvest — cut the top and give the lowers another 5–7 days.

**Lighting for inspection:** use natural light or a white LED.
Colored grow lights (especially blurple) distort trichome
color. Turn the plant light off and use a flashlight.

## Picking your window

There's no single "correct" harvest — there's a window of
about two weeks, and where you cut inside it tunes the effect:

- **~15% clear / 85% milky / trace amber** — the early edge.
  Bright, active, cerebral effect. Best for sativa-leaning
  strains where you want the energetic profile
- **Mostly milky / 10–20% amber** — the classic target. Full
  potency, balanced effect. Most growers cut here
- **30%+ amber** — the late edge. Heavier body effect, more
  sedative. Some prefer it for night use or pain management

## Supporting signals (use alongside trichomes, not instead)

| Signal | Ready indicator |
| --- | --- |
| Pistils | 70–90% darkened and curled in |
| Fade | Fan leaves yellowing hard from bottom up |
| Calyx swell | Buds feel dense and firm, not fluffy |
| Resin production | Buds smell loudest they ever have |
| Water intake | Plant starts drinking less |

**None of these alone tells you when to cut.** A plant can
have 90% brown pistils and still have clear trichomes — cut
it now and you lose potency. Trichomes are the final arbiter.

## The common timing mistakes

**Cutting based on the breeder's timeline.** Most "8-week"
strains actually finish in 9–10. Sativas regularly run 11–12.
The number on the seed pack is an optimistic minimum. Trust
trichomes.

**Checking only once.** Start checking trichomes at the
breeder's stated finish time, then check **every 2–3 days**.
The transition from cloudy to amber accelerates — you can go
from "one more week" to "slightly past" in five days.

**Panicking about yellow leaves.** Late-flower fade (bottom
leaves yellowing) is normal and expected. The plant is
spending its last nitrogen reserves on bud production. Don't
feed harder to stop the fade — you're almost done.

**Waiting for all trichomes to be amber.** At 50%+ amber,
THC has significantly degraded. The bud will be very
sedating — practically narcotic — and you've lost much of
the strain's intended character.

## Harvest day basics

Cut whole branches, not individual buds — they hang better for
drying. Many growers harvest right at lights-on (or at the end
of the dark period) when terpene content is highest.

Trim off the big fan leaves, keep sugar leaves on for a
slower dry, and get branches hanging in your dry space within
the hour. What happens over the next two weeks — drying and
curing — decides whether all this ripeness actually survives
to the jar. See [drying & curing](/guides/drying-and-curing)
for the complete post-harvest playbook.
