Cannabis topicals are applied to the skin — they don't cross the blood-brain barrier, so they **don't produce a high.** They're the most accessible way to use your harvest, even for people who don't consume cannabis internally.

## How topicals work

Cannabinoids (THC, CBD) interact with CB1 and CB2 receptors in the skin. Applied topically, they stay local — they don't reach the bloodstream in meaningful amounts. This is why a 200mg THC salve won't make you feel stoned, but may provide localized comfort.

## Basic cannabis salve recipe

### Ingredients
- **½ cup cannabis-infused coconut oil** (see the Cannabutter guide)
- **¼ cup beeswax pellets** (for hardness — more = firmer)
- **10–15 drops essential oil** (optional — lavender, peppermint, or eucalyptus)
- **1 tbsp vitamin E oil** (optional — extends shelf life)

### Steps

1. Melt infused coconut oil and beeswax in a double boiler over low heat.
2. Stir until fully combined.
3. Remove from heat. Add essential oils and vitamin E if using.
4. Pour into small tins or jars immediately — it sets fast.
5. Let cool completely before capping.

> **Adjust firmness:** More beeswax = harder balm (good for sticks). Less beeswax = softer salve (easier to spread). A 2:1 oil-to-wax ratio is a good starting point.

## Infused massage oil (liquid)

Skip the beeswax entirely:

1. Start with cannabis-infused coconut oil or olive oil.
2. Add 10 drops of essential oil per ounce for scent.
3. Store in a pump bottle.

Liquid at room temperature, ready to use. Coconut oil solidifies below ~76 °F — warm the bottle in your hands or in warm water.

## Lip balm

1. Melt **2 tbsp infused coconut oil** + **1 tbsp beeswax** + **1 tsp shea butter.**
2. Add a few drops of peppermint oil.
3. Pour into lip balm tubes or small tins.
4. Cool and cap.

## Bath soak

1. Mix **¼ cup infused coconut oil** with **1 cup Epsom salt** and **10 drops lavender oil.**
2. Add to a hot bath. Soak for 20+ minutes.
3. The oil disperses in the water and coats skin.

## Tips for better topicals

- **Decarb matters for topicals too.** THCA converts to THC during decarbing, and THC binds skin receptors more effectively.
- **Add CBD flower** to your infusion for broader receptor activation — a 1:1 THC:CBD blend is popular for topicals.
- **Menthol or camphor** (from peppermint/eucalyptus oils) create a warming/cooling sensation that complements the cannabis.
- **Don't use on broken skin** — infused oils on open wounds can sting and isn't what topicals are designed for.

## Shelf life and storage

- Salves and balms: **6–12 months** at room temperature in sealed tins.
- Liquid oils: **3–6 months** refrigerated.
- Add vitamin E to extend shelf life.
- Keep away from direct sunlight and heat.

## What topicals can't do

Topicals **cannot** produce a systemic high. They work locally only. If you want whole-body effects, you need edibles, [tinctures](/guides/cannabis-tinctures), or inhalation. The exception is **transdermal patches** (pharmaceutical-grade, not DIY), which are designed to cross the skin barrier.
