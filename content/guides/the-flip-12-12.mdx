Photoperiod cannabis flowers when nights get long: 12 hours
of uninterrupted darkness tells the plant winter is coming.
Indoors, you are the season. The day you set the timer to
12/12 is the day flowering begins — and the decisions you
make in the next two weeks set the trajectory for everything
that follows.

## Before you flip: the checklist

- **Height check.** Plants stretch 50–100% after the flip
  (some sativa-dominant hybrids stretch even more). If your
  plant is at 60 cm and the light hangs at 120 cm, subtract
  pot height, light clearance, and double the plant height —
  do the math now, not in week 3
- **Canopy even?** The stretch amplifies unevenness. A cola
  that's 10 cm taller than its neighbors in veg will be 20 cm
  taller in flower, blocking light from everything below it.
  Level the table with
  [LST](/guides/low-stress-training-lst) first
- **Healthy?** Fix deficiencies and pests before the flip —
  flowering plants have less capacity to recover, and
  treatments like neem oil can't be used on buds
- **Training done?** All topping and heavy training should be
  at least one week behind you. The plant needs time to
  recover before the hormonal shift
- **Timer tested?** A light leak or a timer glitch during
  flower causes hermaphroditism and re-veg. Verify the
  off-period is truly off

## Darkness is non-negotiable

The flowering signal is unbroken darkness. During the 12
off-hours:

- No tent peeks with the room light on
- Tape over power LEDs inside the tent (fans, power strips,
  humidity controllers — even small indicator lights count)
- Check for light leaks: sit inside the closed tent during
  lights-off — after 5 minutes your eyes should see
  **nothing**
- Zip the tent fully. A 1-inch gap at the intake port is
  enough to interrupt the signal

**What light leaks cause:** the plant receives a
contradictory photoperiod signal. Mild leaks delay or stall
flower development. Severe or repeated leaks trigger
hermaphroditism — the plant produces pollen sacs alongside
pistils, seeding itself and your entire tent. See the
[hermaphrodite prevention guide](/guides/hermaphrodite-prevention)
for the full list of stressors.

## What changes at the flip

| Factor | Veg | Flower |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Light cycle | 18/6 | 12/12 |
| Humidity | 50–65% | 40–50%, dropping to 35–45% late flower |
| Nutrients | High N (3-1-2) | Transition → bloom (1-3-2) |
| Temperature | 24–28 °C | 22–26 °C; cooler nights help color |
| Training | Active topping/LST | Maintain ties only — no new cuts |

**Nutrient transition timing:** don't switch to bloom feed
on day one of 12/12. The plant still wants nitrogen through
the stretch — it's building new vegetative growth at its
fastest rate of the entire grow. Start transitioning to
bloom feed around week 2–3 of 12/12, when vertical growth
stops and bud sites start stacking pistils. For detailed
bloom feeding, see the
[flower feeding strategy](/guides/flower-feeding-strategy).

## The stretch (weeks 1–3 of flower)

This is the final growth spurt. The plant can gain 50–100%
of its pre-flip height in just two weeks. What to do:

- **Keep training ties maintained.** Growth is fast enough to
  pop loose ties in 48 hours
- **Raise the light as needed.** The canopy is closing the
  gap daily — check distance every 2–3 days
- **Spread tops apart** as they elongate. The stretch is your
  last chance to create space between bud sites for airflow
  and light penetration
- **Don't panic** at the speed. The stretch is normal and
  expected — it stops by week 3 in most cultivars

By the end of the stretch, pistils cluster into real bud
sites and the plant's energy pivots from height to flower
mass. You'll know it's over when the daily height gains
stop and the canopy "freezes."

### Managing a stretch that's too much

If the plant is approaching the light despite your
planning:

1. **Supercrop the tallest colas** — bend them at 90° to
   bring them to canopy level. See
   [supercropping](/guides/supercropping-advanced)
2. Raise the light as high as possible, then dim slightly
   if needed (less heat = less stretch)
3. Increase the day/night temperature difference — cooler
   nights slow stretch
4. As a last resort, lower light hours to 11/13 (this
   slows growth slightly but controls height)

## Sexing: catch males in week 1–2

If you're growing from regular (non-feminized) seed, check
every plant at the nodes from day 7 after the flip:

- **Females** show white hairs (pistils) — two thin threads
  emerging from the calyx at each node
- **Males** show small round pollen sacs like tiny bunches
  of grapes, on short stems at the nodes

**One open male sac seeds your whole tent.** Remove males
the day you spot them — carefully, in a garbage bag, without
shaking. For a visual reference and more detail, see the
[sexing guide](/guides/sexing-cannabis-plants).

## The first two weeks of flower: a timeline

| Day | What's happening |
| --- | --- |
| 1–3 | No visible change. Plant is receiving the signal |
| 4–7 | Stretch begins. Rapid vertical growth |
| 7–10 | Pre-flowers appear at nodes (pistils or pollen sacs) |
| 10–14 | Stretch at full speed. Pistil clusters forming |
| 14–21 | Stretch slows and stops. Bud sites defined |

## Common flip mistakes

**Flipping too early.** A small plant in a big pot produces
a small harvest — more veg time (within reason) always means
more yield. If you flip at week 3 of veg, expect one main
cola and not much else.

**Flipping too late.** The plant doubles in flower. If you
veg until the canopy fills 80% of vertical space, the
stretch pushes it into the light. Always flip at 50% of
usable height or less.

**Changing everything at once.** The flip is stressful
enough — don't also transplant, defoliate, and switch
nutrients on the same day. Change the timer, and adjust
everything else gradually over the next two weeks.

**Ignoring the stretch.** "I'll deal with it later" turns
into bleached top colas two weeks later. Stay on top of
light height daily during the stretch.
