You can't improve bud after harvest, but you can absolutely ruin it. A rushed dry destroys terpenes; a bad cure grows mold. The difference between harsh grass-smelling bud and smooth, loud, jar-worthy medicine is entirely in these two-plus weeks.

## Drying: the 60/60 rule

Hang branches in darkness at **60 °F / 60 % RH** (15–16 °C / 55–62 %) with gentle indirect airflow. The target is a **slow, even 7–14 day dry**. Fast-dried bud (3–4 days) smells like hay because the chlorophyll never broke down and the volatile terpenes boiled off.

- Hang whole branches; buds shouldn't touch each other
- Fan pointed at a wall, never at the buds
- Darkness matters — UV degrades THC
- No dehumidifier blasting; you're braking, not stopping

**The done test:** small stems *snap* instead of folding, and the outside of buds feels dry while retaining sponginess inside. If big stems snap too, you've over-dried (fixable — see below).

## Curing: the part most people skip

Curing is a slow, controlled finish of the dry inside glass, where remaining moisture redistributes and chlorophyll breaks down. It's what turns "decent" into "excellent."

1. Trim your dried buds and fill **glass jars ~75 % full** — the headspace is working room
2. Target **58–62 % RH inside the jar**. A $2 mini hygrometer in each jar removes all guesswork
3. **Burp daily for the first two weeks:** open each jar for a few minutes to exchange air and release moisture
4. Weeks 3–4: burp every few days
5. From week 4: it's cured. Store cool and dark; quality holds for months

| Jar RH | Meaning | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Above 65 % | Mold risk — act now | Leave jar open 2–4 h, re-check |
| 58–62 % | Perfect cure zone | Continue burping schedule |
| Below 55 % | Drying out | Add a 62 % humidity pack |

**The smell test at every burp:** the jar should smell increasingly like the strain. If a jar ever smells of ammonia or wet hay, the buds were jarred too wet and anaerobic bacteria are at work — take everything out and give it 24 hours of open-air drying before re-jarring.

## Rescuing an over-dried harvest

Crumbly, over-dried bud can be partially rehydrated in the jar with a 62 % humidity pack over several days. Terpenes that boiled off don't come back, but texture and smoke quality recover substantially. (The orange-peel trick works too, but it's a mold gamble — humidity packs are $1.50.)

## The timeline nobody wants to hear

Chop day + 10 days drying + 3 weeks curing = **a month between harvest and properly finished medicine**. Impatience at this stage is the single most common reason home-grown bud disappoints. You waited four months; wait four more weeks.
