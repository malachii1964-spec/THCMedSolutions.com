You can't improve bud after harvest, but you can absolutely ruin it.
A rushed dry destroys terpenes; a bad cure grows mold. The
difference between harsh grass-smelling bud and smooth, loud,
jar-worthy flower is entirely in these two-plus weeks.

## Why drying matters more than you think

Fresh-cut cannabis is roughly 75% water. As that water leaves,
two parallel processes determine the final product:

**Chlorophyll breakdown.** Chlorophyll is what makes fresh
plant material taste harsh and "green." It breaks down slowly
during a controlled dry — but only if the dry is slow enough.
Speed-dry in three days and the chlorophyll stays locked in.

**Terpene preservation.** Terpenes are volatile — they
evaporate at low temperatures. The slower and cooler the dry,
the more terpene profile you keep. This is why a properly dried
and cured bud smells ten times louder than one that was dried
in a cardboard box with a fan.

## Drying: the 60/60 rule

Hang branches in darkness at **60 °F / 60% RH** (15–16 °C /
55–62%) with gentle indirect airflow. The target is a **slow,
even 7–14 day dry**. Fast-dried bud (3–4 days) smells like
hay because the chlorophyll never broke down and the volatile
terpenes boiled off.

### Setting up the dry room

- **Darkness** — UV degrades THC. A closet, a tent with the
  light off, or a dark room all work
- **Hang whole branches** — don't trim yet. The extra leaf
  material slows the dry, and you want slow. Leave 12–18
  inches between branches; buds shouldn't touch
- **Airflow, not wind** — one small fan pointed at a wall (never
  at the buds) keeps air circulating. Stagnant air breeds mold;
  direct breeze dries the outside too fast while the inside
  stays wet
- **Temperature control** — if your space is warmer than 70 °F
  (21 °C), terpenes start evaporating noticeably faster. Use
  the AC, dry at night, or pick a basement
- **Humidity control** — too dry (below 45% RH) and the outside
  dries in a day while the core stays wet. Too humid (above
  70%) and you're growing mold. A small humidifier or
  dehumidifier may be needed, but don't aim it at the buds

### The done test

Small stems **snap** instead of folding, and the outside of
buds feels dry while retaining sponginess inside. Big stems
should still have some flex — if big stems snap too, you've
over-dried (fixable — see below).

A common mistake is testing only by feel: use your nose too.
At 7 days, crushed leaves should smell green but not "wet."
If you jar too early, you'll know at the first burp (ammonia
smell = jarred too wet).

## Trimming: wet vs dry

**Wet trim** (at chop) is faster and easier — fan leaves peel
off cleanly and the scissors reach everywhere while the plant
is fresh. But it exposes more bud surface to air, which speeds
drying.

**Dry trim** (after drying) preserves more terpenes because the
extra leaf wraps around the bud during drying, slowing
evaporation. The trade-off is sticky, more tedious trimming.

Most home growers: **remove fan leaves at chop, do the detail
trim after the dry.** This is the best compromise between
terpene preservation and practical speed. See the
[trimming guide](/guides/trimming-wet-vs-dry) for the
full comparison.

## Curing: the part most people skip

Curing is a slow, controlled finish of the dry inside glass,
where remaining moisture redistributes and chlorophyll breaks
down. It's what turns "decent" into "excellent."

1. Trim your dried buds and fill **glass jars ~75% full** —
   the headspace is working room
2. Target **58–62% RH inside the jar**. A $2 mini hygrometer
   in each jar removes all guesswork
3. **Burp daily for the first two weeks:** open each jar for a
   few minutes to exchange air and release moisture
4. Weeks 3–4: burp every few days
5. From week 4: it's cured. Store cool and dark; quality holds
   for months

| Jar RH | Meaning | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Above 65% | Mold risk — act now | Leave jar open 2–4 h, re-check |
| 58–62% | Perfect cure zone | Continue burping schedule |
| Below 55% | Drying out | Add a 62% humidity pack |

**The smell test at every burp:** the jar should smell
increasingly like the strain. If a jar ever smells of ammonia
or wet hay, the buds were jarred too wet and anaerobic bacteria
are at work — take everything out and give it 24 hours of
open-air drying before re-jarring.

## Long-term storage

Properly cured flower stores well for 6–12 months if you
control three things:

- **Temperature** — cool (below 70 °F / 21 °C); heat
  degrades cannabinoids. A closet in a temperature-controlled
  room is fine; a freezer works for very long storage but
  makes trichomes brittle
- **Light** — darkness. UV converts THC to CBN (sleepy, not
  what most growers want). Use opaque jars or store in a dark
  space
- **Humidity** — 55–62% with a two-way humidity pack (Boveda
  62% or Integra Boost). The pack absorbs or releases moisture
  to hold the target; replace when it gets crunchy

Mason jars work. So do CVault stainless containers or any
airtight vessel. Plastic bags do not — they create static that
knocks trichomes off the bud.

## Rescuing an over-dried harvest

Crumbly, over-dried bud can be partially rehydrated in the jar
with a 62% humidity pack over several days. Terpenes that
boiled off don't come back, but texture and smoke quality
recover substantially. The orange-peel trick works too, but
it's a mold gamble — humidity packs are $1.50.

**If you catch it early** (stems snap but buds still have
some give): jar immediately with a humidity pack and wait
48 hours. Moisture from the stems and core redistributes
into the outer bud.

**If it's dust-dry**: the smoke will be harsh and the terpene
profile is diminished. A humidity pack will restore the
physical texture, but the flavor damage is permanent. Next
time, dry slower.

## The five mistakes that ruin the post-harvest

1. **Drying too fast** — cardboard box, fan on the buds, warm
   room. Always the #1 cause of "my bud smells like hay"
2. **Jarring too early** — stems fold, not snap. Within 24
   hours the jar reads 70%+ and you're growing mold
3. **Not burping** — sealed jars with wet bud go anaerobic;
   the ammonia smell is bacteria digesting your harvest
4. **Storing in heat or light** — degrades THC to CBN and
   cooks off terpenes over weeks
5. **Plastic bags** — static strips trichomes; bags aren't
   airtight; plastic can impart flavor over time

## The timeline nobody wants to hear

Chop day + 10 days drying + 3 weeks curing = **a month
between harvest and properly finished flower**. Impatience
at this stage is the single most common reason home-grown bud
disappoints. You waited four months; wait four more weeks.
