Left alone, cannabis grows one dominant top (cola) and a lot of smaller stuff below it. Training breaks that dominance so the plant grows *many* equal colas — a flat, even canopy that catches far more light and yields dramatically more. LST (low-stress training) bends; this guide *cuts*. It's called high-stress training (HST), and topping is the gateway.

## Topping — the fundamental cut

**Topping** = snipping off the main growing tip. The plant responds by promoting the two side shoots below the cut into **two new main colas**. Do it again to each, and you get four. Again, eight.

**How:**
1. Wait until the plant has **4–6 nodes** and is healthy (never top a struggling plant).
2. With clean scissors, cut the main stem **just above the 4th or 5th node**, removing the top tip.
3. The two branches at that node become your two new mains.
4. Recover 3–5 days, then top those for four, and so on.

**Only top in vegetative growth**, and stop ~1–2 weeks before you flip to flower so the plant can recover.

## FIM — the lazy cousin

**FIM** ("F*** I Missed") is a sloppier top: instead of a clean cut below the tip, you pinch/cut about **80% through the newest growth at the very tip**, leaving a bit behind. The plant often responds with **3–4 new tops** instead of 2. Less precise, less stressful, and faster — but messier and less predictable than topping.

- **Top** for clean, symmetrical structure (best for manifolds/SCROG).
- **FIM** for quick bushiness with minimal effort.

## Mainlining / manifolding — the symmetrical machine

Mainlining builds a perfectly **symmetrical hub** so every cola gets identical energy — the highest-effort, highest-reward HST structure.

1. Top the seedling down to a **single node** (drastic — trust it).
2. The two shoots from that node become your **two "mainlines."** Remove everything else below.
3. Train those two out flat and let each grow, then **top each at the same node count** → 4 colas.
4. Repeat symmetrically → 8, 16 colas, all fed equally from one central hub.

The result is a menorah-like plant where every bud is a top. It costs 1–2 extra weeks of veg but produces a stunningly even, heavy canopy.

## Combine with LST for the pro finish

HST and LST aren't rivals — you top to *create* tops, then LST (bend and tie) to *spread* them into a flat, even table. Standard indoor recipe:

| Week | Move |
| --- | --- |
| Veg 2–3 | Top above node 4–5 (or start a manifold at node 1) |
| Veg 3–5 | Top again as branches develop; begin LST-ing them outward |
| Veg 5–6 | Spread all tops into a flat canopy |
| ~1 wk pre-flip | Last adjustments, then hands off |

## Rules so you don't hurt the plant

- **Sterile, sharp cuts** — ragged tears invite infection.
- **Healthy plants only** — HST is stress; a sick plant can't afford it.
- **Vegetative only** — never top in flower.
- **Autoflowers: be careful.** Autos run on a fixed clock and can't recover lost time — most auto growers stick to gentle LST, or top very early (node 3–4) at most.
- **One technique at a time to learn** — top a plant, watch what happens, then add complexity.

Master topping and you've unlocked the single biggest yield lever in the tent. Everything else — SCROG, manifolds, even canopies — is built on it.
