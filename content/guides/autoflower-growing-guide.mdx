Autoflowers flower on their own timeline regardless of light schedule. No 12/12 flip, no clones, no photoperiod management — just plant, grow, and harvest in 8–12 weeks. They're the fastest path from seed to smokeable bud, and modern autos rival photoperiods in potency and yield.

## How autos are different

| | Photoperiod | Autoflower |
|--|------------|------------|
| **Flowering trigger** | Light schedule change (12/12) | Internal clock (age-based) |
| **Total time** | 14–20+ weeks | 8–12 weeks seed to harvest |
| **Light schedule** | 18/6 veg → 12/12 flower | 18/6 or 20/4 the entire run |
| **Cloning** | Yes | No (clones would auto on the mother's clock) |
| **Plant size** | Medium to large | Compact to medium |
| **Yield per plant** | Higher | Lower (but more harvests per year) |
| **Forgiving?** | More recovery time | Less — stress costs a bigger % of the grow |

## The auto lighting schedule

Most growers run autos on **18/6** (18 hours on, 6 off) from seed to harvest. Some run 20/4 for maximum photosynthesis. A few run 24/0, though most plants benefit from some dark period.

**18/6 is the sweet spot.** More light than photos get in veg, all the way through flower. Your electric bill stays sane and the plant gets rest.

## Choosing a pot size

Autos don't have time to recover from transplant shock. **Plant directly into the final container.**

- **3-gallon:** Compact plants, tight spaces, SOG setups.
- **5-gallon:** The standard. Balances root room with manageable plant size.
- **7-gallon:** Maximum yield potential for larger auto varieties.

Don't use huge pots thinking it'll make the plant bigger — autos have a genetic size ceiling. Excess soil just stays wet and causes problems.

## Feeding autos

Autos are more sensitive to nutrients than photos. They're smaller, grow faster, and have less recovery time.

- **Start at half strength** of whatever the nutrient label says.
- **Don't feed for the first 2 weeks** in pre-amended soil (Ocean Forest, etc.).
- **Coco growers:** Start feeds at week 1 but go light — 300–400 PPM at first.
- **Living soil:** Many autos finish without bottled feeds if the soil is properly amended.

> The #1 auto mistake is overfeeding. A nutrient-burned auto can't recover fast enough — the damage becomes the yield.

## Training autos (what works, what doesn't)

**DO:**
- **LST (Low Stress Training):** Bend and tie from week 2–3. This is the primary yield-boosting technique for autos.
- **Leaf tucking:** Tuck large fan leaves under bud sites instead of removing them.

**MAYBE (experienced only):**
- **Topping:** Some modern autos handle one early top (at the 4th–5th node) if the plant is vigorous and healthy. If it's slow or stressed, skip it.

**DON'T:**
- **Heavy defoliation:** Autos don't have time to regrow leaf mass. Remove only leaves that block airflow to bud sites.
- **Mainlining/manifolding:** Too much stress for the short timeline.
- **Supercropping:** Risk vs. reward is bad with a 70-day window.

## The auto timeline (typical 10-week variety)

| Week | Stage | What's happening |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | Seedling | First leaves emerge. Don't feed, don't overwater. |
| 2 | Early veg | First real leaves. Start gentle LST if vigorous. |
| 3 | Late veg | Growth accelerates. Begin light feeding. |
| 4 | Pre-flower | Pistils appear. The plant shows sex. Growth stretch begins. |
| 5 | Early flower | Bud sites form. Switch to bloom nutrients. |
| 6 | Mid flower | Buds fatten. Increase airflow, watch humidity. |
| 7 | Mid flower | Trichomes developing. Buds getting dense. |
| 8 | Late flower | Buds ripening. Pistils darkening. |
| 9 | Ripening | Most pistils brown/orange. Check trichomes daily. |
| 10 | Harvest | Mostly cloudy trichomes with some amber. Chop. |

Your plant doesn't read the schedule. Some varieties finish in 8 weeks, some in 12. **Watch the trichomes, not the calendar.**

## Perpetual auto harvest

Because autos don't need a light change, you can run plants at different stages in the same tent under the same lights. Start a new seed every 3–4 weeks and you'll harvest every 3–4 weeks once the pipeline fills — perpetual harvest with one tent, one timer.

## Common auto mistakes

1. **Transplanting** — Plant in the final pot from seed. Every transplant costs days you don't have.
2. **Overwatering seedlings** — Small plant in a big pot means wet soil for days. Water a small circle around the stem.
3. **Overfeeding** — Half strength, always. Less is more with autos.
4. **Training too late** — LST works in weeks 2–4. By week 5 the structure is set.
5. **Harvesting by the calendar** — "It says 8 weeks" doesn't mean YOUR plant is done at 8 weeks. Check trichomes.
