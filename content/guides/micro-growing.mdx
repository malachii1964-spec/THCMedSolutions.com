Not everyone has a spare room or a closet for a 4×4 grow tent. Maybe
you're in an apartment, maybe you share space, maybe you just want a
small personal supply without a large setup. Micro growing is the answer
— producing quality cannabis in spaces as small as 2 square feet.

This isn't about compromise. A well-designed micro grow can produce
2–4 ounces every 3–4 months from a space smaller than a mini fridge.
That's enough for personal use, and the quality matches any full-size
tent grow.

## What counts as "micro"

| Setup | Footprint | Plant count | Yield per cycle |
|-------|----------|-------------|----------------|
| 2×2 tent | 4 sq ft | 1–2 | 2–4 oz |
| Single-plant cabinet | 2–3 sq ft | 1 | 1–2 oz |
| Space bucket (5-gal) | 1 sq ft | 1 | 0.5–1.5 oz |
| PC case grow | ~1.5 sq ft | 1 | 0.5–1 oz |
| 2×4 tent | 8 sq ft | 2–4 | 4–8 oz |

A 2×4 isn't truly "micro" but shares many of the same constraints.
This guide focuses on 2×2 and smaller.

## The 2×2 tent: the sweet spot for micro

A 2×2×5-foot tent is the most practical micro setup. It's large enough
to grow 1–2 plants comfortably with real equipment, small enough to fit
in a closet corner, and cheap ($50–80 for the tent itself).

### Equipment list

| Item | Recommendation | Cost |
|------|---------------|------|
| Tent | 2×2×5 or 2×2×4 (any brand) | $50–80 |
| Light | 100–150W LED quantum board | $50–100 |
| Fan/filter | 4" inline fan + carbon filter | $60–80 |
| Pot | 2–3 gallon fabric pot (1–2 pots) | $5–10 |
| Timer | Mechanical or digital outlet timer | $10–15 |
| Hygrometer | Basic digital temp/humidity | $10 |
| **Total** | | **$185–295** |

### The light for a 2×2

You need 30–50 actual watts per square foot for cannabis in flower.
A 2×2 is 4 square feet, so 120–200W of quality LED.

Good options:
- **Mars Hydro TS 1000** (150W) — fits a 2×2 perfectly, good spectrum
- **Spider Farmer SF1000** (100W) — slightly under-powered for dense
  flower but works for 1 plant
- **HLG 135W Rspec** — premium efficiency, runs cool
- **Viparspectra P1000** (100W) — budget-friendly

Don't over-light a 2×2. A 300W panel in this space will cook your
plants — there's not enough volume to dissipate the heat, and the
intensity at the canopy will cause bleaching.

### Airflow in tight spaces

The biggest challenge in micro grows is **heat and humidity management**.
A small, enclosed space with a grow light heats up fast and traps
moisture.

- A 4" inline fan with a carbon filter is the minimum. It pulls heat
  out, manages humidity, and scrubs odor
- In summer, you may need the fan running at full speed. In winter,
  you can often run it on a speed controller at 50–75%
- Don't use a clip fan inside a 2×2 — there's no room. The inline
  exhaust creates enough passive air movement through the intake vent

### Pot size matters

In a 2×2, a 3-gallon fabric pot is ideal for a single plant. Two
plants fit in 2-gallon pots. Don't use anything larger than 3 gallons
— there's not enough floor space, and the root mass won't have room
to develop without crowding.

Smaller pots mean faster dry-back cycles, which means watering more
frequently. In a 2-gallon pot during flower, you may water daily or
even twice daily in coco.

## Strain selection for micro grows

Not every strain works in a 2×2. You need genetics that stay short
and don't stretch too much during flower.

### What to look for

- **Indica-dominant or compact hybrids** — naturally shorter, bushier
  growth. Sativas can triple in height during the stretch and outgrow
  your tent
- **Autoflowers** — bred for compact size. Most autos stay under 3
  feet. They're the #1 choice for micro grows because you don't need
  to manage height as aggressively
- **Known short strains** — look for breeder descriptions mentioning
  "compact," "short," or "good for small spaces"

### Strains that excel in micro

Good bets include:
- Northern Lights (compact indica, forgiving)
- White Widow (manageable height, good yield for size)
- Blueberry (short, dense, classic indica)
- Any auto from Mephisto, Night Owl, or Barney's Farm (bred for
  compact growth)
- Critical (fast, compact, reliable)

### Strains to avoid in micro

- Tall sativas: Haze varieties, Amnesia, Super Silver Haze
- Heavy stretchers: Sour Diesel, Jack Herer
- Anything described as "vigorous" or "needs head room"

## Training for micro: it's not optional

In a full-size tent, training increases yield. In a micro grow, training
is mandatory — without it, the plant outgrows the space and burns
against the light.

### The micro training stack

1. **LST from day one (after node 4–5)** — bend the main stem
   horizontal. In a micro grow, you're training for flatness more than
   branching. The canopy should never be more than 8–10 inches from the
   pot rim

2. **Topping once at node 4–5** — creates two main colas instead of
   one, giving you a wider, flatter canopy. In a 2×2 with one plant,
   one top is usually enough. Don't top more than twice — each top adds
   recovery time, and in small pots the plant doesn't have the root mass
   to support 8+ colas

3. **Aggressive tucking** — instead of removing fan leaves (which
   reduces energy production), tuck them under the branches to expose
   bud sites. In micro, every leaf matters for photosynthesis — don't
   defoliate heavily

4. **Supercrop if it gets too tall** — if a branch is about to hit the
   light, pinch and bend it. This is the emergency height control tool.
   See our [training comparison guide](/guides/training-techniques-compared)

### The height budget

In a 2×2×5 tent:
- 5 feet total = 60 inches
- Minus light + driver: -8 inches
- Minus hanging hardware: -2 inches
- Minus pot height: -10 inches (3-gal fabric pot)
- Minus light-to-canopy distance: -12 inches minimum
- **Available plant height: ~28 inches**

That's 28 inches from soil to the tallest bud. A topped and LSTd
indica can fill that easily. An untrained sativa will blow past it by
week 3 of flower.

## The space bucket

A space bucket is a DIY micro grow chamber built from stacked 5-gallon
buckets. It's the ultimate stealth grow — costs $50–100 to build,
fits in a closet, and produces 0.5–1.5 oz per run.

### Basic build

1. **Bottom bucket:** holds the pot (1–2 gallon). Drill drainage holes
2. **Spacer rings:** cut the bottoms out of additional buckets and stack
   them on top to add height as the plant grows
3. **Lid:** holds the light. LED strips (SMD 5630 or equivalent)
   arranged in a circle around the inside of the lid. Or a small 50W
   quantum board
4. **Intake:** 2–3 holes (2") in the bottom bucket with PC fans pulling
   air in
5. **Exhaust:** a PC fan in the top bucket or lid pushing air out through
   a small carbon filter (a PC carbon filter or DIY activated carbon tube)
6. **Interior:** lined with reflective mylar or flat white paint

### Space bucket limitations

- Very low yield ceiling (1–1.5 oz max)
- Heat management is challenging with so little air volume
- Only one plant, and it must be trained flat
- Autoflowers only (photoperiods get too tall)
- Fun as a project, but a 2×2 tent is more practical for the same
  effort

## Nutrient management in small pots

Small pots dry out faster and buffer less. This changes how you feed.

### The key differences

- **Feed more often, at lower concentration.** A 2-gallon pot in coco
  may need feeding daily at 50–75% strength instead of every-other-day
  at full strength
- **Monitor runoff EC more frequently.** Salt buildup happens faster in
  small volumes of medium. Check runoff EC every 2–3 feeds
- **pH drift is more noticeable.** Less medium means less buffer. Small
  pH deviations in your feed water show up faster in the root zone

### Coco vs. soil in micro

**Coco** is popular for micro grows because it's lighter, drains faster,
and allows more frequent feeding without overwatering. The trade-off is
more daily attention — coco should never dry out completely.

**Soil** is more forgiving for growers who can't check daily. A good
soil with perlite in a 3-gallon fabric pot gives you 2–3 day watering
cycles even in flower.

## Common micro-grow mistakes

### 1. Too much plant for the space

One well-trained plant in a 2×2 will out-yield two overcrowded plants
every time. Overcrowding creates humidity pockets, blocks airflow, and
guarantees mold risk. In micro: one plant, trained wide and flat.

### 2. Not managing heat

A 150W LED in a 2×2×5 tent with inadequate exhaust can push temps above
95°F in summer. Size your exhaust fan for the worst-case scenario (peak
summer temps + light at full power). A speed controller lets you turn it
down in winter.

### 3. Using too-large pots

A 5-gallon pot in a 2×2 takes up the entire floor and leaves no room
for access. The root system can't fill a 5-gallon pot in the short veg
time typical of micro grows. Use 2–3 gallon pots maximum.

### 4. Growing a sativa in a closet

Respect genetics. An untrained Haze in a 2×2 will touch the light in
week 2 of flower. Choose compact strains and train aggressively.

### 5. Skipping odor control

"It's just one plant" — until week 5 of flower, when a single plant
in an enclosed space smells like a dispensary through two walls. A 4"
carbon filter is mandatory for stealth grows, even with one plant.

## The micro grow calendar

For a single autoflower in a 2×2 (the simplest micro approach):

| Week | Stage | What to do |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Germination | Paper towel → Rapid Rooter → solo cup |
| 1 | Seedling | Low light, high humidity, barely water |
| 2 | Seedling → veg | Transplant to 2–3 gal pot, increase light |
| 3–4 | Veg | Begin LST as soon as node 4 appears |
| 4–5 | Pre-flower | Plant shows sex. Continue LST. Light at 75% |
| 5–8 | Flower | Light at 100%, manage humidity, train flat |
| 8–10 | Late flower | Reduce nutes, monitor trichomes |
| 10–11 | Harvest | Chop when 10–20% amber trichomes |
| 11–13 | Dry/cure | Dry 7–10 days, jar cure 2+ weeks |

Seed to smoke in ~13 weeks. 1–3 ounces of quality flower from 4 square
feet. That's micro growing.
