The moment you switch your light cycle from 18/6 (or 20/4) to 12/12,
a cascade of hormonal changes begins inside the plant. The first
visible result is dramatic: the plant stretches. In 10–14 days, most
cannabis strains will grow 50–200% taller than they were at the flip.

This is the transition period — not quite veg, not yet full flower.
Managing it well sets up the rest of your bloom. Managing it poorly
leaves you with stretched plants hitting the light, an uneven canopy,
and nutrient problems that haunt you for weeks.

## What happens during the stretch

### The hormonal shift

When cannabis receives 12 hours of uninterrupted darkness, it
triggers the production of florigen — a flowering hormone. But the
plant doesn't immediately stop growing vegetatively. Instead, it
enters a growth sprint:

- **Days 1–3:** no visible change. The hormonal shift is happening
  internally
- **Days 4–7:** rapid vertical growth begins. Internodes elongate.
  The plant may grow 2–4 inches per day
- **Days 8–14:** stretch peaks, then slows. Pre-flowers (white
  pistils at the nodes) become visible
- **Days 15–21:** stretch ends. The plant's energy shifts fully to
  bud production

### How much stretch to expect

| Genetics | Typical stretch | Example strains |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Indica-dominant | 50–75% height increase | Northern Lights, Granddaddy Purple, Bubba Kush |
| Hybrid | 75–150% height increase | Blue Dream, Wedding Cake, Gelato |
| Sativa-dominant | 150–200%+ height increase | Haze strains, Durban Poison, Super Silver Haze |

**Example:** a plant that's 12 inches tall at the flip may finish
the stretch at 18 inches (indica) to 36 inches (sativa). Plan
your flip height accordingly.

## When to flip

### The height rule

Flip when the plant is **50% of the maximum height your space
allows.** If your light can be 24 inches from the canopy and your
tent ceiling is 60 inches, your maximum plant height is 36 inches.
Flip at 18 inches.

Adjust for genetics:
- **Indica-dominant:** flip at 50–60% of target height
- **Sativa-dominant:** flip at 33–40% of target height (they stretch
  more)
- **First time growing a strain?** flip conservative (earlier). You
  can always veg longer next time

### When the canopy is ready

The flip isn't just about height. Before flipping:
- **Training should be complete** — topping, manifolding, and major
  LST should be done at least 7 days before the flip. The plant
  needs time to recover before the stretch
- **The canopy should be level** — an uneven canopy means uneven
  stretch, and the tallest colas will be too close to the light
- **Defoliation** — do a light defoliation 2–3 days before the flip
  to open up the canopy. The plant will fill back in during the
  stretch

## Managing the stretch

### Keep training during weeks 1–2

The stretch is your last opportunity for significant canopy
management. During this period:

- **Continue LST:** bend stretching branches outward to maintain an
  even canopy. Check ties daily — growth is fast enough to snap tight
  ties
- **Supercrop tall branches:** if one cola is racing ahead of the
  others, a gentle supercrop (bending until the inner fibers crack)
  will slow it and redirect growth hormones to the lower branches.
  Do this before week 2 ends — after that, stems harden and become
  brittle
- **Adjust the light height:** raise the light to maintain the
  recommended distance as the plant grows toward it. Falling behind
  on light height during the stretch causes light burn on the
  tallest colas

### Week 1 defoliation

Many growers do a strategic defoliation at the start of flower:
- Remove large fan leaves that block light to interior bud sites
- Remove any leaves touching the soil or pot (moisture trap, pest
  harbor)
- **Don't strip the plant.** Remove 15–20% of fan leaves max. The
  plant needs those leaves to fuel the stretch

A second, heavier defoliation at the end of the stretch (day 14–21)
is common and effective. See our
[defoliation guide](/guides/defoliation-guide).

## Nutrient transition

### When to switch to bloom nutrients

Don't switch immediately at the flip. The plant is still growing
vegetatively during the stretch — it needs nitrogen to fuel that
growth.

**Recommended transition:**
| Days after flip | Feed |
|----------------|------|
| 1–5 | Full veg nutrients (or a 50/50 veg/bloom mix) |
| 6–10 | 50/50 veg and bloom nutrients |
| 11–14 | Full bloom nutrients |

### Common transition mistakes

- **Switching to bloom nutrients on day 1:** the plant yellows during
  the stretch because it's not getting enough nitrogen for the rapid
  growth. Bloom nutrients are low-N
- **Continuing full veg nutrients through week 3:** the plant gets
  nitrogen toxic as it enters actual flowering. The shift should be
  gradual, not abrupt or delayed

### Cal-mag during transition

Maintain your cal-mag regimen through the stretch — calcium demand
increases during rapid growth. This is a common deficiency point. See
our [cal-mag guide](/guides/cal-mag-complete-guide).

## Environmental adjustments

### Temperature

Keep flower temps at 75–80°F during the day, 68–72°F at night. The
stretch benefits from slightly warmer daytime temps (more growth
hormone activity). After the stretch ends, you can begin cooling
toward the 72–76°F range that's optimal for mid-to-late flower.

### Humidity

Begin lowering humidity during weeks 1–2:
- **End of veg:** 55–65% RH
- **Week 1 of flower:** 50–60% RH
- **Week 2 of flower:** 45–55% RH
- **Week 3+:** 45–50% RH

The reduction is gradual. Don't crash humidity from 60% to 40% on
flip day. See our
[VPD guide](/guides/temperature-humidity-vpd).

### Light intensity

You can increase light intensity during the stretch — the plant can
handle more light as it grows larger. Many growers run their LED at
75% during veg and ramp to 100% during the first two weeks of flower.

Monitor for light stress: if leaf tips curl upward (taco) or
bleaching appears on the top leaves, dim slightly or raise the light.

## Watching for problems

### Pre-flowers and sex identification

By the end of week 2, you should see pre-flowers — small pistils
(white hairs) emerging from the nodes. This confirms the plant is
female and flowering.

**Check for male traits:** round, smooth pollen sacs (no pistils)
at the nodes indicate a male or hermaphrodite plant. Remove
immediately. See our
[hermaphrodite prevention guide](/guides/hermaphrodite-prevention).

### Light leaks

The first two weeks are the most sensitive period for light leaks.
The plant is transitioning its hormonal state, and any light during
the dark period can:
- Delay or prevent flowering
- Cause hermaphroditism
- Create an uneven stretch (confused growth patterns)

Check your tent for light leaks during the dark period. Cover
indicator LEDs, seal zipper gaps, and never open the tent during
lights-off.

## Common mistakes

1. **Not accounting for stretch height** — the plant you flipped at
   24 inches is now 48 inches and touching the light. Flip earlier
   than you think you need to
2. **Stopping training at the flip** — the stretch is the last
   window for canopy management. Use it. After week 2, the stems
   harden and you can't bend them
3. **Switching nutrients too abruptly** — the transition from veg to
   bloom should take 7–14 days, not happen overnight
4. **Panicking about the growth rate** — the stretch is normal and
   expected. A plant doubling in height in two weeks isn't wrong.
   That's what cannabis does
5. **Not raising the light** — the plant is growing toward the light
   at 2+ inches per day. If you don't adjust the light height daily,
   the top colas will bleach
