The most common seedling mistake isn't overwatering — it's wrong
light. Too much light and the cotyledons bleach white or curl under.
Too little and the seedling stretches into a tall, weak stem that
falls over under its own weight. The window between "not enough"
and "too much" is narrower for seedlings than at any other stage.

This guide gives you the exact numbers: PPFD, light height, and
photoperiod from germination through the transition to vegetative
growth.

## Why seedlings need less light

A seedling has almost no leaf surface area. The cotyledons (the first
round leaves) are tiny solar panels — they can only absorb so much
light before the photosynthetic machinery overloads. An adult cannabis
plant in flower thrives at 800–1000 µmol/m²/s PPFD. A seedling at
that intensity would bleach and die within a day.

As the plant grows leaves, it can handle more light. The progression
is gradual:

| Stage | Age | PPFD target | DLI target |
|-------|-----|-------------|------------|
| Emerging (cotyledons only) | Day 1–3 | 100–200 µmol/m²/s | 6–10 mol/m²/day |
| First true leaves | Day 4–7 | 200–300 µmol/m²/s | 12–16 mol/m²/day |
| Second node | Day 8–14 | 300–400 µmol/m²/s | 16–22 mol/m²/day |
| Transition to veg | Day 14+ | 400–600 µmol/m²/s | 22–35 mol/m²/day |

DLI (Daily Light Integral) is the total light a plant receives per
day. PPFD × hours × 0.0036 = DLI. A seedling running 200 PPFD for
18 hours gets a DLI of 12.96 — right in the sweet spot for the first
true leaves.

## Light height by fixture type

You probably don't have a PAR meter for your first grow. That's fine —
use distance from the canopy as a proxy.

### LED panels (most common)

| Wattage | Seedling height | PPFD at that height |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| 100W (2×2 panel) | 30–36 inches | ~150–200 µmol/m²/s |
| 200W (3×3 panel) | 36–42 inches | ~150–250 µmol/m²/s |
| 300W+ (4×4 panel) | 42–48 inches | ~150–200 µmol/m²/s |

**The rule:** start with the light at the highest position your tent
allows and dim it to 25–40% power if your light has a dimmer. That
combination of maximum distance + low power gives gentle, even light
that won't overwhelm seedlings.

### CFL bulbs

If you're using CFL bulbs for seedlings (a valid, cheap approach):
- **23W CFL (100W equivalent):** 3–4 inches from the top of the
  seedling
- **2–4 bulbs in a cluster:** covers a small seedling tray
- CFLs produce almost no heat at reasonable distances and very low
  PPFD, so they're nearly impossible to overdo

### T5 fluorescent fixtures

- **4-bulb T5HO fixture:** 8–12 inches above seedlings
- These are the traditional nursery light — even coverage, low
  intensity, minimal heat

## Photoperiod: how many hours

### Photoperiod (regular) seeds

Run **18 hours on / 6 hours off** from day one. The 6 hours of
darkness isn't strictly required during the seedling stage, but it
establishes the rhythm you'll maintain through veg and saves
electricity. Some growers run 20/4 or even 24/0 for seedlings — both
work, but the gains over 18/6 are minimal and the extra electricity
cost isn't justified.

### Autoflower seeds

Run **20 hours on / 4 hours off** from day one. Autoflowers don't
need a dark period to trigger flowering (they flower based on age,
not light cycle), and the extra 2 hours of daily light compounds
into meaningful growth over a 70–80 day lifecycle. Some auto growers
run 24/0, but most strains benefit from at least 4 hours of darkness
for recovery.

## Signs your light is wrong

### Too much light (light stress)

- **Cotyledons curl downward** — the edges fold under, making the
  leaf look like a taco
- **Bleaching** — leaves turn pale yellow or white, starting from the
  center, spreading outward
- **Stunted growth** — the seedling stops growing vertically and the
  leaves thicken but don't expand
- **Red or purple stems** — can indicate light stress (though genetics
  and temperature also cause this)

**Fix:** raise the light 6 inches and/or dim 10–20%. Check again in
24 hours. Seedlings recover quickly from mild light stress if caught
early.

### Too little light (stretching)

- **Tall, thin stem** — the seedling is reaching for light. The
  internodal spacing (distance between nodes) is excessive
- **Leaning** — the seedling leans toward the light source
- **Pale green color** — not enough light for vigorous chlorophyll
  production
- **Floppy stem** — the stem can't support itself and the seedling
  falls over

**Fix:** lower the light 6 inches and/or increase intensity by 10%.
If the seedling has already stretched, you can bury the stem up to
the cotyledons when you transplant — it will root along the buried
stem.

## The first 14 days: a schedule

### Days 1–3: emergence

The seed has cracked and the cotyledons are emerging from the soil.

- **Light:** on at 25–30% intensity, maximum height (or CFL at 4
  inches)
- **Schedule:** 18/6 (photo) or 20/4 (auto)
- **Watch for:** stretching (stem growing tall without opening
  cotyledons). If the seedling is more than 2 inches tall before
  cotyledons open, it's stretching — lower the light

### Days 4–7: first true leaves

The serrated cannabis leaves appear between the cotyledons.

- **Light:** increase to 35–50% intensity, or lower the light 4–6
  inches
- **Schedule:** same — 18/6 or 20/4
- **Watch for:** leaf curl (too much light) or continued stretching
  (not enough). The stem should be short and sturdy, and the first
  true leaves should be broad and flat

### Days 8–14: second and third nodes

The plant now has 2–3 sets of true leaves and is entering early
vegetative growth.

- **Light:** increase to 50–60% intensity, or lower the light
  another 4–6 inches
- **Schedule:** same — 18/6 or 20/4
- **Watch for:** the transition from "seedling" to "vegetative" is
  gradual. When the plant has 4+ nodes and the leaves are growing
  visibly wider each day, you can begin increasing light toward full
  veg intensity (400–600 PPFD)

### Day 14+: transition to veg

- **Light:** ramp to 60–75% intensity over the next week
- **Target:** 400–600 PPFD by day 21
- **Don't jump straight to full power** — increase 10% per day and
  watch for stress

## Using a phone app as a PAR meter

Dedicated PAR meters cost $300–500. Phone apps (Photone, Korona) use
the phone's light sensor to estimate PPFD. They're not laboratory-
grade, but they're accurate enough for seedling management:

1. Download Photone (iOS/Android) — the free version works for
   LED and CFL
2. Select your light type (LED, fluorescent, HPS)
3. Hold the phone face-up at canopy height (top of the seedling)
4. Read the PPFD value
5. Adjust light height or dimmer until you hit your target

**Accuracy caveat:** phone sensors are better at relative measurement
(is this spot 2× brighter than that spot?) than absolute measurement.
Use the numbers for guidance, not as gospel. If the app says 200
µmol/m²/s and the seedling looks healthy, the number is close enough.

## Common mistakes

1. **Blasting seedlings with full power** — many new growers unbox
   a 300W LED and run it at 100% intensity 18 inches above a seedling.
   That's 3–4× more light than the seedling can handle. Start low,
   ramp up
2. **No light at all for the first few days** — some guides suggest
   keeping seedlings in the dark until they emerge. Once the seed
   cracks and the white tap root is visible, it needs light the moment
   it breaks the soil surface (not before)
3. **Inconsistent light schedule** — a seedling doesn't need a
   specific schedule as desperately as a flowering plant, but
   consistency helps. Set a timer and don't fiddle with it
4. **Forgetting to raise the light as the plant grows** — the light
   stays fixed but the plant grows toward it. A 30-inch gap on day 1
   becomes a 24-inch gap by day 7 — enough to cause stress. Check
   the gap every few days
5. **Using an HPS/MH for seedlings** — high-intensity discharge
   lights produce massive heat and PPFD. They work for seedlings only
   if hung very high (4+ feet) and are overkill. Use a small LED or
   CFL for the first 2 weeks, then switch to the HPS for veg
