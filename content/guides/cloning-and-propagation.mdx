A clone is a cutting off a living plant that grows its own roots — a genetic copy of the parent. Master cloning and you get **free plants forever**, perfect consistency, and a head start every grow (a rooted clone skips germination and seedling fragility). It's the technique that turns one great plant into an endless supply.

## Why clone

- **Free plants.** No seed cost, ever, once you have a keeper.
- **Guaranteed female** (if the mother is female) — no sexing, no herm surprises.
- **Identical genetics.** Same height, flower time, and effect every run — dial a strain in once, repeat it forever.
- **Speed.** Rooted clones are weeks ahead of seeds.

## What you need

- A healthy donor plant in **vegetative** growth (never clone a flowering plant if you can help it — it re-vegs slowly and stresses)
- Clean, sharp scissors or a scalpel
- **Rooting gel or powder** (Clonex is the standard)
- A rooting medium: rockwool cubes, Rapid Rooter plugs, or even a cup of water
- A humidity dome + tray (holds the ~90% humidity cuttings need)
- Gentle light (a weak LED or T5; clones don't want intensity yet)

## Taking the cutting

1. Pick a healthy branch with **2–3 nodes**, ideally lower/side growth.
2. Cut at a **45° angle** just below a node with clean scissors (more surface = more rooting).
3. Strip the lower leaves, leaving 2–3 leaves at the top. Trim big fan leaves in half to cut water loss.
4. **Immediately** dip the cut end in rooting gel (air in the stem = a dead cutting).
5. Insert into moistened rockwool/plug (or water) and place under the dome.

## The two weeks that root it

- **Humidity 90%+** to start (the dome), venting a little more each day
- **Temp 72–77 °F** — a heat mat under the tray speeds rooting
- **Light gentle**, 18/6
- **Keep the medium moist**, not soaked
- Mist inside the dome the first few days; taper off
- **Roots appear in 7–14 days.** When white roots poke out, harden off (open the dome gradually) and transplant.

**Don't fuss.** The #1 clone killer is impatience — pulling them to "check roots" or over/under watering. Set the environment and leave them alone.

## Water cloning — the dead-simple method

No dome, no gel needed: cut, drop the stem in a cup of clean room-temp water on a windowsill, change the water every couple of days. Roots in 1–3 weeks. Less reliable at scale, perfect for one or two cuttings and total beginners.

## Keeping a mother plant

A **mother** is a plant kept permanently in veg (18/6, never flipped to flower) that you take cuttings from indefinitely. One healthy mother in a corner supplies every future grow with free, identical clones.

- Keep her **vegetative** — never let her flower.
- Feed steadily, prune to keep her bushy and full of cutting sites.
- Refresh with a new mother (clone of a clone) every so often to keep vigor up.

## The payoff

Seeds are how you *find* a keeper. Cloning is how you *keep* it. Once one plant impresses you — great yield, flavor, resilience — take clones and a mother, and you never start from zero again. It's the single biggest jump from "grower" to "gardener with a program."
