A space bucket is a self-contained cannabis growing system built
from stacked 5-gallon buckets. It occupies less than 2 square feet
of floor space, costs under $100 to build, and can produce 1–2
ounces of dried flower per cycle. It's not going to compete with
a 4×4 tent on yield, but for apartment growers who need absolute
discretion and have zero space for traditional setups, it's the
smallest functional grow possible.

## What you'll build

A completed space bucket is:
- A 5-gallon bucket (the growing chamber) topped with spacer
  rings (for height) and a lid containing LED lighting
- Light-tight (no light escapes to reveal the grow)
- Ventilated (fan + carbon filter for airflow and odor control)
- Reflective interior (for light efficiency)
- Modular (spacer rings add height as the plant grows)

Total dimensions: ~14" diameter × 24–36" tall. Fits in a closet,
under a desk, or in a corner.

## Build instructions

### Step 1: Prepare the main bucket

The bottom bucket is the growing chamber:
1. Cut a 3-inch hole in the side near the bottom for the intake
   fan. The fan should blow air INTO the bucket
2. Line the entire interior with adhesive mylar. Smooth it flat —
   wrinkles reduce reflectivity and create hotspots
3. Drill 4–6 drainage holes in the bottom (in case of water
   overflow from the inner pot)

### Step 2: Build the spacer rings

Spacer rings give your plant room to grow vertically:
1. Take the extra 5-gallon buckets and cut off the bottom and top
   rim, leaving a cylinder approximately 10–12 inches tall
2. Line the interior with mylar
3. These stack on top of the main bucket. Start with 1 spacer and
   add more as the plant grows

### Step 3: Build the lid (lighting unit)

The lid holds the top light and exhaust fan:
1. Mount the UFO or COB LED to the underside of the bucket lid
   using bolts or thermal adhesive. Center it
2. Cut a hole in the side of the TOP bucket (which sits inverted
   as the lid enclosure) for the exhaust fan. The fan should blow
   air OUT of the bucket
3. Attach the carbon filter to the exhaust fan intake side (inside
   the bucket). A simple DIY filter: stuff activated carbon between
   two layers of pantyhose and duct-tape it over the fan intake

### Step 4: Install side lighting

LED strips on the inside walls of the spacer rings dramatically
improve lower-bud quality:
1. Adhere warm white + cool white LED strips in vertical runs
   inside each spacer ring
2. Wire them in parallel to the 12V power supply
3. The combination of top and side light reduces larf and improves
   yield on a short, bushy plant

### Step 5: Wire and seal

1. Route all power cables through a single drilled hole at the
   base (seal with silicone to prevent light leaks)
2. Check for light leaks: turn on the lights, turn off the room
   lights, and look for escaping light. Seal any leaks with black
   duct tape or silicone
3. Test the fans — air should flow in through the bottom fan,
   through the plant canopy, and out through the top exhaust

## Growing in a space bucket

### Strain selection (critical)

Space buckets have severe height limitations (24–36 inches total
growing space, less with the light and pot). Strain choice matters
more here than in any other grow setup:

**Best choices:**
- **Autoflowers** — short lifecycle, compact size, no photoperiod
  requirements. Top picks: Northern Lights Auto, White Widow Auto,
  Lowryder, Critical Auto
- **Indica-dominant photoperiods** — naturally short and bushy.
  Northern Lights, Hindu Kush, Afghan Kush
- **Micro grows** specifically bred for small spaces

**Avoid:**
- Any sativa-dominant strain (too tall)
- Any strain that's known for stretching
- Large-yielding strains (they need space to produce those yields)

See our [autoflower guide](/guides/autoflower-complete-guide) and
[genetics guide](/guides/genetics-phenotypes).

### Training (mandatory)

In a space bucket, training isn't optional — it's the difference
between a harvest and a plant that crashes into the light:

1. **Top once** at the 4th or 5th node to keep height down and
   create multiple colas
2. **LST aggressively** — bend all branches outward and down,
   securing them to the pot edge. The goal is a flat, wide canopy
   that fills the bucket diameter without growing tall
3. **Lollipop** the bottom 1/3 of the plant — in such a small
   space, lower growth gets no light and produces nothing usable

See our
[training schedule](/guides/progressive-plant-training).

### Light schedule

**Autoflowers:** run 20/4 or 18/6 from seed to harvest. No
photoperiod change needed.

**Photoperiods:** run 18/6 during veg (3–5 weeks is usually
enough in a bucket), then flip to 12/12 for flower. The plant
should be no more than 8–10 inches tall at the flip — it will
double during the stretch.

### Environment management

Space buckets have tight environmental margins:

**Temperature:** the small enclosed volume heats up quickly. The
LED and exhaust fan are your temperature management system. If
temperatures exceed 85°F:
- Increase exhaust fan speed or upgrade to a larger fan
- Run lights during the cooler part of the day (overnight)
- Remove a spacer ring to increase airflow volume

**Humidity:** the small volume also traps humidity. During flower,
humidity above 60% in such a tight space is a mold invitation.
The exhaust fan is your primary humidity control.

**Airflow:** the intake and exhaust fans create positive pressure
from bottom to top. This provides fresh CO2, removes heat, and
moves air around the plant to strengthen stems. Adequate airflow
is critical in such a small volume.

### Watering

A 3-gallon pot in a space bucket dries out relatively quickly
under lights. Check daily — lift the pot to feel weight. In
flower, you may need to water every 1–2 days.

Don't overwater — in the confined bucket space, excess water has
nowhere to go quickly, and high humidity from wet soil in an
enclosed volume promotes mold.

### The grow timeline (autoflower)

| Week | Stage | Bucket action |
|------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | Germination + seedling | 1 spacer ring, low light |
| 2–3 | Early veg | Begin LST, increase light |
| 4 | Late veg / top | Top at node 4–5, add spacer if needed |
| 5–6 | Pre-flower | Continue LST, begin bloom feed |
| 7–9 | Flower | Check for mold daily, maintain airflow |
| 10–11 | Late flower | Trichome checks, reduce feed |
| 12 | Harvest | Chop and dry |

### Expected yield

**Realistic yield from a space bucket:** 0.5–2 ounces dried per
cycle. Variables:
- Light quality: a 100W LED produces more than a 50W LED
- Strain: some genetics produce more in small spaces
- Training: effective LST can double yield vs. untrained
- Grower experience: first bucket grow is a learning curve

Don't expect tent-level yields. A space bucket is about growing
personal-use cannabis in the smallest possible footprint, not
about maximizing production.

## Upgrades

Once you've done a successful bucket grow, common upgrades:

**Better lighting:** upgrade from LED strips to a purpose-built
quantum board (100W actual draw) designed for small spaces. This
can increase yield by 30–50%.

**Smart environmental control:** a small temperature/humidity
sensor (like a SensorPush) inside the bucket with an alert on
your phone helps you catch temperature spikes before they damage
the plant.

**Automated watering:** a small drip system on a timer prevents
the daily "lift and check" routine, useful if you travel.

**Dual-bucket system:** build two space buckets and run them on
staggered timelines for a perpetual harvest — one in veg while
the other is in flower. See our
[perpetual harvest guide](/guides/perpetual-harvest-setup).

## Limitations (be honest with yourself)

- **Yield:** 0.5–2 oz maximum. If you consume more than an ounce
  per month, a space bucket won't keep up
- **Strain limitations:** you can only grow short, compact plants
- **Maintenance:** daily monitoring is more critical than with a
  tent because the environment margins are tighter
- **Heat:** powerful LEDs in a small enclosed space generate heat
  that's hard to manage in summer
- **Smell:** a DIY carbon filter helps but won't eliminate odor
  as effectively as a proper inline fan + carbon filter
- **Not scalable:** if you want more yield, you need a tent, not
  more buckets (at some point, a 2×2 tent is simpler and more
  effective than 3 space buckets)

## When to upgrade to a tent

If any of these apply, you've outgrown the space bucket:
- You want more than 2 oz per cycle
- You want to grow sativa or large-hybrid strains
- You're spending more time managing the bucket environment than
  enjoying the grow
- You have the space for a 2×2 or 2×4 tent

A 2×2 tent ($50–70), a 150W LED ($80–120), and a 4-inch inline
fan + carbon filter ($60–80) gives you a setup that's still
compact but produces 2–6 oz per cycle with far less hassle. See
our [equipment guide](/guides/first-grow-equipment) and
[tent setup guide](/guides/grow-tent-setup-guide).

## Common mistakes

1. **Using a white bucket** — white buckets let light through.
   Algae grows in the soil and root zone. Use BLACK buckets only,
   or wrap white buckets in black duct tape
2. **Insufficient exhaust** — heat builds fast in 5 gallons.
   Upgrading the exhaust fan is the single most impactful
   improvement for bucket growing
3. **Growing a tall strain** — a Sour Diesel in a space bucket
   will crash into the light within weeks. Choose short genetics
4. **Skipping training** — an untopped, untrained plant in a
   bucket produces one small cola. Training is mandatory for any
   reasonable yield
5. **Light leaks** — light escaping the bucket compromises stealth
   and can cause hermaphroditism in photoperiod plants. Check for
   leaks with all external lights off
6. **Neglecting pH** — the same pH rules apply in a bucket as in
   a tent. Check pH every watering. See our
   [pH guide](/guides/ph-for-beginners)
