You grew it, dried it, cured it. Now you want to eat it. Making
edibles from homegrown cannabis is straightforward once you
understand two things: decarboxylation (the heat activation
step most beginners skip) and dosing (the math that prevents
you from eating 10× more than you intended).

This guide covers the science and process of turning dried
cannabis flower into consistently dosed, properly activated
edibles.

## Step 1: Decarboxylation (mandatory)

### Why you must decarb

Raw cannabis contains THCA, not THC. THCA is non-psychoactive
— eating raw flower produces no high. Heat converts THCA to
THC through a process called decarboxylation (removing a
carboxyl group from the molecule).

When you smoke or vaporize cannabis, decarboxylation happens
instantly from the heat. When making edibles, you need to
decarb the flower BEFORE infusing it into fat — otherwise
your butter will contain mostly inactive THCA.

### How to decarb

**Oven method (most common):**
1. Preheat oven to 240°F (115°C)
2. Break flower into small pieces (pea-sized, not ground to
   powder — powder burns easily)
3. Spread evenly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper
4. Bake for 40–60 minutes
5. The flower should turn from green to golden-brown and
   smell toasted
6. Remove and let cool before handling

**The temperature-time relationship:**

| Temperature | Time | Decarb completeness |
|-------------|------|-------------------|
| 220°F (105°C) | 60–90 min | ~85–90% conversion |
| 240°F (115°C) | 40–60 min | ~95–97% conversion (optimal) |
| 250°F (121°C) | 30–40 min | ~95% but risk of terpene loss |
| Above 300°F | — | THC begins degrading to CBN — avoid |

**240°F for 40 minutes is the standard.** This maximizes THC
conversion while minimizing THC degradation and terpene
evaporation.

### Decarb tips

- **Don't grind before decarbing.** Finely ground flower has
  more surface area, which increases terpene loss and the
  risk of burning
- **Use an oven thermometer.** Home ovens are notoriously
  inaccurate — verify your oven actually reaches and
  maintains 240°F
- **Contain the smell.** Decarbing smells strongly. An oven
  bag (turkey bag) tied closed on the baking sheet contains
  most of the aroma. Mason jar decarbing (sealed jar in the
  oven) also works and contains smell completely
- **Don't skip this step.** The most common cause of weak
  edibles is insufficient decarboxylation

## Step 2: Infusion into fat

THC is fat-soluble, not water-soluble. It needs a fat carrier
to be absorbed by your digestive system. The two most common
carriers:

### Cannabutter

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter
- 7–14 grams of decarbed flower (see dosing section)
- 1 cup water (prevents butter from burning)

**Process:**
1. Melt butter in a saucepan with 1 cup of water over low
   heat
2. Add decarbed flower to the butter-water mixture
3. Maintain temperature at 160–180°F (a very low simmer — no
   boiling). If the mixture boils, you're too hot and will
   degrade THC
4. Stir occasionally and maintain low heat for 2–3 hours
5. Strain through cheesecloth into a container. Squeeze the
   cheesecloth to extract all the butter
6. Refrigerate. The butter will solidify on top of the water.
   Remove the solid butter disk, discard the water
7. The butter is ready to use in any recipe

### Infused coconut oil

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup coconut oil (solid or liquid)
- 7–14 grams of decarbed flower

**Process:**
1. Combine coconut oil and decarbed flower in a saucepan or
   slow cooker
2. Heat at 160–180°F for 2–3 hours (slow cooker on "warm"
   or lowest setting)
3. Strain through cheesecloth into a container
4. Let cool. Coconut oil solidifies below 76°F

**Why coconut oil:** it has a higher percentage of saturated
fat than butter, which binds more THC per volume. It's also
vegan, shelf-stable longer, and works in both cooking and
baking.

### Slow cooker method (easiest)

1. Combine fat and decarbed flower in a slow cooker
2. Set to "warm" or lowest setting (check with a thermometer —
   target 160–180°F)
3. Let cook for 4–6 hours, stirring occasionally
4. Strain and store

The slow cooker method is the easiest because it maintains
steady, low temperature without constant attention.

## Step 3: Dosing math

This is where most homemade edibles go wrong. Dispensary
edibles are lab-tested for precise dosing. Homemade edibles
require you to calculate the dose yourself.

### The formula

```
Total THC (mg) = grams of flower × THC% × 10 × decarb efficiency
```

**Example:** 7 grams of flower at 20% THC:
```
7 × 20 × 10 × 0.95 = 1,330 mg total THC in the batch
```

If you infuse that into 1 cup of butter (16 tablespoons):
```
1,330 ÷ 16 = ~83 mg THC per tablespoon
```

If a cookie recipe calls for 4 tablespoons of butter and makes
12 cookies:
```
(4 × 83) ÷ 12 = ~28 mg per cookie
```

### Dosing guidelines

| Dose | Experience level | Expected effect |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 2.5–5 mg | Beginner / low tolerance | Mild relaxation, light euphoria |
| 5–10 mg | Casual user | Moderate effect, standard recreational dose |
| 10–25 mg | Experienced user | Strong effect, significant body and mind |
| 25–50 mg | High tolerance | Very strong, not for beginners |
| 50+ mg | Very high tolerance only | Overwhelming for most users |

**Start low.** If you've never eaten edibles, start at 5 mg
and wait 2 hours before eating more. Edibles take 45–120
minutes to onset and last 4–8 hours. You can always eat more;
you can't eat less.

### The homemade dosing problem

The biggest challenge with homemade edibles is **consistency**.
Even with careful math, the THC distribution in your infused
butter isn't perfectly even — some spoonfuls may be stronger
than others.

**Mitigation:**
- Stir the butter or oil thoroughly before portioning
- Use recipes that distribute the fat evenly (brownies,
  cookies) rather than recipes where fat pooling is possible
- Test-dose a small amount (a teaspoon of butter on toast)
  before committing to a full batch of brownies

### If you don't know the THC percentage

Most homegrown cannabis is in the 15–25% THC range. If you
don't have a lab test:
- Assume 15% THC for conservative dosing (better to be
  under-dosed than over-dosed)
- The calculation with 7 grams at 15%: 7 × 15 × 10 × 0.95 =
  ~1,000 mg total

## Using trim and sugar leaves

You don't have to use prime bud for edibles. Trim (sugar
leaves, small buds, popcorn buds) works well and is a better
use of material that wouldn't be enjoyable to smoke:

| Material | Approximate THC content | Amount per cup of fat |
|----------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Top-shelf flower | 15–25% THC | 7–10 grams |
| Popcorn buds / small buds | 10–18% THC | 10–14 grams |
| Sugar leaves / trim | 5–10% THC | 14–28 grams |
| Fan leaves | 0–2% THC | Not worth using |

Increase the amount of trim proportionally to compensate for
lower THC content. The infusion process is the same.

## Storage

- **Cannabutter:** refrigerator for 2–4 weeks, freezer for 6
  months
- **Infused coconut oil:** room temperature in an airtight
  container for 2–3 months, refrigerator for 6 months
- **Finished edibles:** same shelf life as the base recipe
  (brownies = 1 week room temp, 2 weeks fridge; gummies with
  preservatives = 2–4 weeks)
- **Label everything.** Mark containers clearly with "THC" and
  approximate dosage per serving. Keep away from children,
  pets, and anyone who shouldn't accidentally eat them

## Common mistakes

1. **Skipping decarboxylation** — raw flower contains THCA,
   not THC. Without decarbing, your edibles will be weak or
   inactive. Always decarb at 240°F for 40 minutes before
   infusing
2. **Overheating during infusion** — boiling the butter/oil
   destroys THC and terpenes. Keep temperature at 160–180°F.
   A thermometer is essential
3. **Not doing the dosing math** — "I'll just put a bunch in
   and see" leads to either weak edibles (waste of flower) or
   dangerously strong edibles (miserable 8-hour experience).
   Calculate the dose before cooking
4. **Not waiting long enough** — edibles take 45–120 minutes
   to kick in. Eating a second dose at 30 minutes because
   "it's not working" leads to consuming double the intended
   dose. Wait at least 2 hours
5. **Using fan leaves** — fan leaves contain negligible THC.
   Use sugar leaves, trim, or flower. Fan leaves add plant
   taste without meaningful potency
