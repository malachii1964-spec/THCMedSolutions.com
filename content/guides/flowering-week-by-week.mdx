Flower is where new growers get nervous: leaves yellow, smells change, and every forum thread says something different. Here's the week-by-week of a typical 8-week photoperiod strain — what's normal, what needs action.

## Weeks 1–3: the stretch

**Normal:** rapid vertical growth (plants can double), white pistils clustering at nodes, thirst climbing week over week.

**Action:** maintain training ties, keep nitrogen up until the stretch slows, raise lights to hold your distance target.

**Not normal:** pollen sacs (remove males/herms immediately), stretch beyond double (light too far away).

## Weeks 3–4: bud set

**Normal:** stretch stops. Bud sites become distinct puffballs of pistils. First trichomes frost the buds and nearby leaves. The smell arrives — carbon filter time.

**Action:** fully transition to bloom nutrients (1-3-2 or similar). Drop humidity below 50 %. Defoliate the interior lightly: big fan leaves that block bud sites, one or two per branch per week, no more.

**Not normal:** pistils browning already (usually heat or light stress at the canopy top).

## Weeks 4–6: the swell

**Normal:** buds fatten and stack; trichomes go from sparse to sugar-dusted; some lower fan leaves start to yellow — the plant is cannibalizing nitrogen, exactly on schedule.

**Action:** this is peak feed demand — watch for calcium and magnesium hunger under LED (rusty leaf spots, interveinal yellowing). Support heavy branches with stakes or yoyos before they fold.

**Not normal:** yellowing that starts at the *top*, burnt tips crisping backward (overfeed), or a sudden all-over droop (usually root-zone: check drainage and pot weight).

## Weeks 6–8: ripening

**Normal:** pistils turn orange-brown and curl in; buds go dense; trichomes turn milky; the fade — leaves yellowing bottom-up — accelerates. Terpene profile shifts from sharp to deep.

**Action:** ease off nutrients toward the end (many growers finish on plain pH'd water for the final 7–10 days). Night temps a few degrees cooler bring out color. Start checking trichomes with a loupe every couple of days (see the harvest-timing guide).

**Not normal:** bud rot — grey-brown mush inside dense colas, usually where humidity sits above 55 %. Cut affected colas well below the rot the moment you find it and get RH down *now*.

## Feeding cheat sheet

| Weeks | Feed | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1–2 | Veg feed continues | Plant still building frame |
| 3–5 | Full bloom feed | Peak demand, watch Cal-Mag |
| 6–7 | Bloom at 75 % | Fade beginning is healthy |
| Final 7–10 days | Plain water | Optional but common practice |

Most "8-week" strains actually finish in 9–10. The calendar is a suggestion; trichomes are the truth.
