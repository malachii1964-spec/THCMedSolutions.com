Sea of Green (SOG) is the opposite of the training-heavy approaches
like SCROG, mainlining, or LST. Instead of growing a few plants for
many weeks and training them to fill the canopy, SOG grows many small
plants with almost no training. You skip the extended veg, skip the
topping, skip the tying — each plant produces a single main cola, and
the collective canopy of those single colas fills the space.

The trade-off is straightforward: more plants, less time per plant,
faster turnaround, but you need a steady supply of clones or seeds.

## How SOG works

### The concept

1. Fill the growing space with as many plants as it can hold at the
   target density (typically one plant per square foot)
2. Veg minimally — just 1–3 weeks, enough for the plant to establish
   roots and a few nodes
3. Flip to 12/12 early. Each plant develops one main cola with
   minimal side branching
4. Harvest, replace, repeat

### Why it works

A cannabis plant's main cola receives the most light and produces the
densest bud. In a traditional grow, you train the plant to create
multiple colas of similar quality. In SOG, you don't bother — you
just grow more plants, each with one cola.

The math is simple:
- 1 trained plant producing 8 colas in 14 weeks (6 veg + 8 flower)
- vs. 8 untrained plants each producing 1 cola in 10 weeks (2 veg +
  8 flower)

The SOG finishes 4 weeks faster and produces roughly equivalent total
yield. Over a year, the faster turnaround means more harvest cycles,
which means more total production from the same space.

## Setup

### Plant density

| Space | Plants (SOG) | Plants (SCROG/LST) |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 2×2 (4 sq ft) | 4–9 | 1–2 |
| 3×3 (9 sq ft) | 9–16 | 2–4 |
| 4×4 (16 sq ft) | 16–25 | 4–6 |
| 4×8 (32 sq ft) | 25–36 | 6–10 |

The standard SOG density is one plant per square foot. Experienced
SOG growers push to 1.5–2 plants per square foot with some strains,
but overcrowding increases humidity problems and restricts airflow.

### Containers

- **1–2 gallon pots** — SOG plants are small. They don't need the
  root mass of a 5-gallon pot. Smaller pots mean more plants fit in
  the space
- **Square pots** pack tighter than round pots (no wasted corner
  space)
- **Fabric pots** are fine but round fabric pots waste edge space.
  Rigid square pots optimize for SOG

### Clones vs. seeds

**Clones (strongly recommended):** SOG works best with genetically
identical plants. Clones from the same mother grow at the same rate,
stretch the same amount, and finish at the same time. This uniformity
is what makes SOG practical — you harvest the entire batch at once
instead of having some plants ready two weeks before others.

**Seeds:** possible but less ideal. Seeds produce variable
phenotypes, meaning different heights, different maturation rates,
and an uneven canopy. If you use seeds, choose a highly stabilized
strain (IBL or heavily worked F4+ line) to minimize variation.

**Autoflowers:** a popular SOG option. Since autos flower based on
age rather than light cycle, you can run them on 20/4 for the
entire cycle (more light = more yield). Auto SOG doesn't require a
separate veg space.

## The SOG timeline

### Week 1: Root establishment

- Root fresh clones or germinate seeds in small containers (solo cups
  or 1-inch Rapid Rooters)
- Light: 18/6 at moderate intensity
- Goal: healthy root system and 2–3 nodes

### Week 2: Transplant and pre-flip

- Transplant into final containers (1–2 gallon)
- Arrange in the growing space at target density
- Continue 18/6 for 3–7 more days to let roots establish in the new
  container
- No training, no topping, no LST

### Week 3: Flip to 12/12

- Switch lights to 12/12
- The plants will stretch 50–150% in height over the next 2–3 weeks
- Each plant develops a single main cola with minimal side branching
  (because there was no topping to break apical dominance)

### Weeks 3–10: Flower

- Manage as you would any flowering plant: appropriate nutrients,
  humidity control, and monitoring
- The canopy should be a uniform field of single colas at roughly the
  same height
- Remove any lower growth (lollipop) from the bottom 1/3 of each
  plant — these won't receive enough light in the dense canopy

### Week 10–11: Harvest

- Harvest the entire batch at once (uniform genetics = uniform
  maturation)
- Clean the space, replace with the next batch of rooted clones
- Repeat

### Total cycle time

From clone cutting to harvest: 10–12 weeks (vs. 14–18 weeks for a
trained grow). This allows 4–5 harvests per year instead of 3.

## SOG and perpetual harvest

SOG enables the most efficient perpetual harvest system:

1. **Veg space:** a small area (2×2 tent) under 18/6 for rooting
   clones and vegging for 2 weeks
2. **Flower space:** the main grow area under 12/12
3. **Every 2–3 weeks:** move a batch from veg to flower. Harvest the
   oldest batch in flower. Start new clones in veg

With a 9-week flowering strain, you can harvest every 3 weeks by
running three batches in flower at staggered stages. This requires
managing multiple stages simultaneously, but the output is consistent
rather than feast-or-famine.

## What to grow in SOG

### Ideal SOG characteristics

- **Short flowering time (7–9 weeks):** faster turnaround = more
  cycles per year
- **Strong apical dominance:** you want one main cola, not a bushy
  plant with many side branches
- **Compact structure:** short, stocky plants fit the density
  requirement better than tall, stretchy sativas
- **Uniform genetics:** clones from a proven mother, or heavily
  stabilized seeds

### Recommended strains

| Strain | Why it works for SOG |
|--------|---------------------|
| Northern Lights | Short, dense, fast (7–8 week flower), strong main cola |
| Afghani | Pure indica, minimal branching, fast |
| White Widow | Moderate height, heavy main cola, reliable |
| Critical Mass | Heavy yield per cola, 7–8 week flower |
| AK-47 | Strong apical dominance, moderate height |
| Auto varieties | No light cycle change needed, consistent timing |

### Strains to avoid for SOG

- Very stretchy sativas (Haze, Jack Herer) — too tall, too branchy
- Heavily branching hybrids (GSC, Gelato) — designed to be topped
  and trained, not grown as single colas
- Extremely long-flowering strains (12+ weeks) — defeats the speed
  advantage of SOG

## Legal considerations

SOG involves growing many plants simultaneously. In jurisdictions
with plant count limits, this can be a legal issue:

- **New York (home grow, 2025):** 6 plants per person (12 per
  household). SOG with 16 plants in a 4×4 exceeds this limit
- **Check your local plant count limits** before choosing SOG as
  your method. You may need to use SCROG (fewer plants, more
  training) to stay within limits

This is a real constraint. Don't build a 25-plant SOG in a 6-plant-
limit state.

## Common mistakes

1. **Overcrowding** — more plants ≠ more yield. Beyond the optimal
   density, plants shade each other and humidity skyrockets. Stick
   to one plant per square foot maximum
2. **Using seeds instead of clones** — genetic variation from seeds
   creates an uneven canopy with different harvest times. Use clones
   for consistency
3. **Vegging too long** — SOG plants should flip to 12/12 within
   2–3 weeks. Extended veg creates plants too large for the tight
   spacing
4. **Not lollipopping** — in a dense SOG, lower growth gets zero
   light. Remove it to redirect energy to the main cola and improve
   airflow
5. **Ignoring humidity** — a 4×4 with 16 transpiring plants generates
   significant moisture. Run the exhaust fan accordingly and monitor
   RH closely during flower
