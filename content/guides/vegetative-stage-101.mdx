Flower gets the glory, but veg decides the yield. Every
branch, node, and root you build now is infrastructure the
plant will load with bud later. A plant that enters flower
small, rootbound, or unhealthy doesn't magically fix itself
— the ceiling was set in veg.

## The environment

| Factor | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Light cycle | 18/6 |
| Temperature | 24–28 °C lights on, 20–22 °C off |
| Humidity | 50–65 % RH |
| Water pH | 6.0–6.8 (soil), 5.5–6.1 (coco/hydro) |
| PPFD | 400–600 µmol/m²/s |

Photoperiod plants stay in veg as long as the light cycle
stays at 18/6 — that's your throttle. More veg time means a
bigger plant and a bigger harvest, at the cost of calendar
time and space. Most indoor growers veg for 4–8 weeks
depending on pot size and training goals.

For the full environment breakdown — temperature, humidity,
and airflow targets by stage — see the
[environmental control guide](/guides/environmental-control-basics).

## Feeding

Veg is nitrogen season. A typical N-P-K for this stage
looks like **3-1-2**.

- Start at **half the label dose** and work up; labels are
  written by people selling nutrients
- Feed, feed, water is a solid soil rhythm — the plain-water
  round flushes salt buildup
- Leaf color is the gauge: deep green is fed, pale
  green-yellow (starting at the bottom) is hungry, dark
  blue-green with clawed tips is overfed
- Track your input EC/PPM with a meter — it removes the
  guesswork from "half strength." See the
  [EC/PPM measuring guide](/guides/ec-ppm-measuring)

### Feeding by week (general soil schedule)

| Veg week | EC target | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1–2 (post-seedling) | 0.4–0.8 | Quarter to half strength |
| 3–4 | 0.8–1.2 | Working up to full veg dose |
| 5–6 | 1.2–1.6 | Full veg, watch for tip burn |
| Pre-flip week | 1.0–1.4 | Slight ease-off before transition |

Coco growers: feed every watering (no plain-water rounds)
and keep EC slightly lower — coco holds nothing on its own.

If leaves are yellowing or showing spots during veg, check
the [nutrient deficiency chart](/guides/nutrient-deficiency-chart)
— but start with [pH](/guides/watering-ph-fundamentals)
before adding anything. Nine times out of ten, the nutrient
is *in* the soil but locked out by wrong pH.

## Transplanting

Roots that hit the pot wall circle it and strangle growth.
Move up pot sizes when leaves span wider than the current
pot — typically solo cup → 1 gal → 3–5 gal final.

**Transplant technique:**
1. Water the plant lightly 12–24 hours before (moist roots
   hold together; soaked ones are heavy and fragile)
2. Fill the new pot with medium, carve a hole the size of the
   old pot
3. Flip the old pot, slide the root ball out by the base —
   never grip the stem
4. Set it in the hole, fill gaps, and water lightly to settle

Expect 1–2 days of stall after transplanting — the roots
are exploring new soil. Don't feed heavy during this window.
For a deeper dive, see the
[transplanting guide](/guides/transplanting-cannabis). If the
plant wilts badly, see
[transplant shock recovery](/guides/transplant-shock-recovery).

## Canopy: the 80/20 of yield

Light only penetrates the top ~30 cm of canopy effectively.
A plant shaped like a Christmas tree wastes most of it. Your
goal is a **flat, wide table of evenly-lit tops**.

- **[Topping](/guides/topping-fim-mainlining)** — snip the
  main growing tip above the 4th–5th node; the plant
  redirects to two main colas instead of one
- **[LST](/guides/low-stress-training-lst)** — bend and tie
  branches outward so light reaches everything
- **[SCROG](/guides/scrog-screen-of-green)** — weave branches
  through a horizontal net to lock the canopy flat
- Remove growth that will never see light: the spindly
  bottom third (this is called lollipopping — see the
  [defoliation guide](/guides/defoliation-guide))

The combination of topping + LST is the standard indoor
recipe: top once at node 4–5, then bend the two resulting
mains outward and keep spreading as the canopy fills. For a
side-by-side comparison of all methods, see
[training techniques compared](/guides/training-techniques-compared).

## Signs veg is going well

- **New node every 2–3 days** — steady vertical and lateral
  growth
- **Thick stem** — you should feel resistance when you gently
  flex it; thin stems mean weak light or no air movement
- **Even color** — consistent green across the canopy without
  yellowing, spotting, or clawing
- **Branching out** — side branches growing aggressively,
  especially after topping or LST

## Common veg-stage problems

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Slow growth, pale leaves | Underfeeding or pH off | Check pH first, then bump feed |
| Dark clawed tips | Nitrogen toxicity | Flush and reduce N. See [nitrogen toxicity guide](/guides/nitrogen-toxicity-excess) |
| Stretchy, tall, thin | Light too high or too dim | Lower light or increase dimmer. See [lighting guide](/guides/lighting-basics-ppfd) |
| Leaf edges curling up | Heat or light stress | Raise light, improve airflow. See [light burn guide](/guides/light-burn-light-stress) |
| Roots poking from drainage | Rootbound — transplant now | Move to next pot size |

## When to flip

Plants roughly **double in height** during the flowering
stretch. Flip to 12/12 when your plant fills about half its
allotted final height and the canopy is even. For most
indoor grows that's week 6–8 of veg.

**Flip readiness checklist:**
- Plant is at 50% of max tent height (after accounting for
  pot + light hang distance)
- Canopy is flat and trained — uneven canopies get worse
  during the stretch
- No active deficiencies or pest issues
- You've finished all topping/training at least a week ago
  so the plant has recovered

See [the flip guide](/guides/the-flip-12-12) for the full
transition checklist and what changes in the tent.
