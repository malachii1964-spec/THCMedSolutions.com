Every cannabis edible on Earth starts with the same thing: **decarbed flower infused into fat.** Master this one recipe and you can make anything — brownies, gummies, salad dressing, pasta sauce, coffee creamer.

## Which fat to use

| Fat | Best for | Smoke point | Shelf life |
|-----|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Unsalted butter** | Baking, toast, sautéing | 350 °F | 2 weeks fridge, 6 months freezer |
| **Coconut oil (refined)** | Gummies, capsules, cooking | 400 °F | 2+ months fridge |
| **Olive oil** | Drizzling, dressings, pasta | 375 °F | 2 months dark pantry |
| **MCT oil** | Tincture-style dosing, capsules | N/A (don't cook) | 3+ months |

All work. **Coconut oil** extracts the most THC because of its high saturated fat content. **Butter** tastes best. Pick based on what you're cooking.

## The ratio

**7–10 grams of decarbed flower per 1 cup (8 oz) of fat.** This produces a moderate-strength infusion — roughly 5–10 mg THC per tablespoon depending on your flower's potency.

- **Lighter:** 3–5g per cup — for microdosing or cooking where you'll use a lot of oil.
- **Stronger:** 14g per cup — for capsules or recipes that use very little fat.

> Start with the standard ratio your first time. You can always use more per serving — you can't un-dose.

## Step-by-step (stovetop method)

1. **Decarb your flower** at 240 °F for 40–45 minutes (see the Edibles guide for detail).
2. **Combine** decarbed flower and fat in a saucepan or double boiler.
3. **Heat on LOW** — maintain 160–180 °F. Never let it boil or simmer hard.
4. **Stir occasionally** for 2–3 hours. The fat will darken.
5. **Strain** through cheesecloth into a jar. Squeeze gently — don't force plant material through.
6. **Cool and store.** Butter solidifies in the fridge; oils stay liquid.

### Don't skip the thermometer

The target is 160–180 °F. Below 150 °F extracts slowly. Above 200 °F degrades THC. A cheap candy thermometer or instant-read is worth it.

## Slow-cooker method (easier, hands-off)

1. Decarb flower as above.
2. Add flower + fat + 1 cup water to a slow cooker.
3. Cook on LOW for 4–6 hours, stirring once an hour.
4. Strain through cheesecloth into a container.
5. Refrigerate — the butter/oil solidifies on top, water sinks to the bottom. Discard the water.

The water pulls out chlorophyll and plant taste, giving you cleaner-tasting butter.

## Straining tips

- **Cheesecloth** (doubled) is the standard. Rubber-band it over a mason jar mouth.
- **Don't squeeze too hard** — you'll push bitter chlorophyll through.
- Discard the spent plant material. It has almost no THC left.

## Storage

- **Fridge:** 2 weeks for butter, 2 months for oils.
- **Freezer:** 6+ months for butter (wrap in silicone molds for portioned cubes).
- **Label everything.** Date, strain, ratio, estimated mg/tbsp.

## Dosing your recipes

If your flower tested at 20% THC and you used 7g per cup:

- 7g = 7,000 mg × 0.20 = 1,400 mg THCA × 0.877 (decarb conversion) ≈ **1,228 mg THC** in the cup
- 1 cup = 16 tablespoons → **~77 mg per tablespoon**
- A brownie recipe using ½ cup butter = ~614 mg ÷ 12 brownies = **~51 mg per brownie**

That's a strong dose. Most people are comfortable at 5–10 mg. **Adjust the ratio or use less infused fat** (mix with regular butter) to hit your target.

## Common mistakes

- **Grinding too fine** — powder goes through the strainer and makes gritty, green, bitter edibles.
- **Overheating** — boiling destroys THC. Low and slow always.
- **Not decarbing** — the #1 reason edibles "don't work."
- **Eating more because it hasn't hit yet** — edibles take 45 min–2 hours to peak. Wait a full 2 hours before re-dosing.
