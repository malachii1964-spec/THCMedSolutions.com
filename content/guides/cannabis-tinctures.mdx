Tinctures are the oldest cannabis preparation — drops under the tongue that hit in 15–30 minutes instead of the 1–2 hour edible wait. They were in the US Pharmacopoeia until 1942. Making them at home is simple.

## Two paths: alcohol vs. oil

| Method | Onset | Duration | Taste | Shelf life |
|--------|-------|----------|-------|------------|
| **Alcohol (ethanol)** | 15–30 min sublingual | 2–4 hours | Strong, herbal-burn | 5+ years |
| **MCT/coconut oil** | 20–45 min sublingual | 3–5 hours | Mild, oily | 2–3 months fridge |

Alcohol extracts more completely and lasts longer. Oil tastes better and avoids alcohol entirely. Both work.

## Green Dragon — classic alcohol tincture

### What you need
- High-proof food-grade ethanol: **Everclear 190 (95%)** is the standard. 151-proof works but extracts slower.
- Decarbed cannabis (7–14g per 2 oz / 60 mL of alcohol)
- Mason jar with lid
- Cheesecloth or coffee filter
- Dark dropper bottles for storage

### Quick method (3 minutes)

1. **Decarb** flower at 240 °F for 40–45 minutes.
2. Place decarbed flower in a mason jar.
3. Pour enough Everclear to just cover the material.
4. **Shake vigorously for 3 minutes.** That's it.
5. Strain through cheesecloth into a dark glass bottle.

> The quick method pulls THC efficiently without extracting as much chlorophyll. Longer soaks (days/weeks) produce stronger but harsher, greener tinctures.

### Long-soak method (traditional)

1. Decarb flower. Place in mason jar.
2. Cover with Everclear. Seal.
3. Store in a dark, cool place for **2–4 weeks**, shaking daily.
4. Strain and bottle.

This produces a more complete extraction — stronger, but darker and more bitter.

## FECO — Full Extract Cannabis Oil

FECO (also called RSO-style) is a super-concentrated tincture where you **evaporate the alcohol**, leaving a thick, dark oil.

1. Make a Green Dragon tincture (long-soak method).
2. Pour into a wide, shallow dish.
3. Place in a **well-ventilated area** (or outdoors) and let the alcohol evaporate naturally. A gentle fan speeds it up.
4. When it stops bubbling and becomes thick like tar, scrape it into a silicone container.

> **NEVER use heat or open flame to evaporate alcohol.** Ethanol vapor is extremely flammable. Ventilation is non-negotiable.

A gram of FECO can contain 600–800+ mg THC. Dose it in **rice-grain-sized** amounts.

## MCT oil tincture (no alcohol)

1. **Decarb** flower at 240 °F for 40–45 minutes.
2. Combine with **MCT oil** in a mason jar (7g per 2 oz oil).
3. Place the jar in a pot of simmering water (double-boiler style).
4. Hold at 160–180 °F for 2–3 hours.
5. Strain through cheesecloth into a dark dropper bottle.
6. Refrigerate.

MCT tinctures absorb faster than other oils sublingual and work well in capsules.

## How to use

- **Sublingual:** Hold drops under your tongue for 60–90 seconds before swallowing. This bypasses digestion and hits faster (15–30 min).
- **In food/drink:** Add drops to coffee, smoothies, or recipes. Onset is like an edible (45–120 min).
- **Topical:** Alcohol tinctures can be applied to skin (for localized discomfort, not intoxication).

## Dosing

Start with **0.25 mL (about 5 drops)** and wait 2 hours. Tincture strength varies wildly based on your flower, ratio, and method. Keep a log of drops → effects until you find your dose.

Label every bottle: date, strain, method, estimated mg/mL.

## Storage

- Alcohol tinctures in dark glass last **years** at room temperature.
- Oil tinctures last **2–3 months refrigerated.**
- Both degrade in light and heat — dark bottles, cool storage.
