Before you buy nutrients, lights, or seeds, you need to answer one
question: what are you growing in? The medium you choose determines
your watering schedule, your feeding approach, how forgiving your setup
is, and how fast your plants grow. It's the most consequential decision
you'll make before your first grow — and unlike most decisions, it's
hard to change mid-cycle.

## The three major options

| Factor | Soil | Coco coir | Hydroponics (DWC) |
|--------|------|----------|------------------|
| Growth speed | Slowest | Fast | Fastest |
| Error forgiveness | Most forgiving | Moderate | Least forgiving |
| Watering frequency | Every 2–4 days | Every 1–2 days | Continuous (reservoir) |
| Nutrient approach | Can be organic or synthetic | Synthetic (usually) | Synthetic |
| pH management | Buffered (forgiving) | Less buffered | No buffer (daily checks) |
| Setup cost | Cheapest | Moderate | Most expensive |
| Maintenance | Lowest | Moderate | Highest |
| Taste/terpenes | Often highest (organic soil) | Excellent | Excellent |
| Yield potential | Good | Very good | Highest |

## Soil: the forgiving path

### What it is

Soil is the natural growing medium — a mix of organic matter, minerals,
and living organisms that provides structure for roots, retains water,
and (in good soil) supplies nutrients naturally.

For cannabis, "soil" usually means a pre-made potting mix like Fox
Farms Ocean Forest, Happy Frog, Roots Organics, or a homemade super
soil. These aren't the dirt in your backyard — they're engineered mixes
of peat moss, perlite, worm castings, bat guano, and other amendments
designed to support cannabis.

### Why growers choose soil

**Buffering:** soil resists pH changes. If your feed pH is slightly
off, the soil's organic matter and microbial activity self-correct to
some degree. In coco or hydro, that same pH drift hits the roots
immediately. This buffering effect is why soil is the most forgiving
medium for beginners.

**Simplicity:** a good pre-amended soil (like Ocean Forest) provides
enough nutrients for the first 3–4 weeks without any additional
feeding. You just add water. When you do start feeding, the stakes
are lower because the soil buffers excess salts.

**Flavor:** many experienced growers believe organic soil produces the
best-tasting cannabis. The slow, microbial nutrient delivery and the
diversity of organic compounds in the soil create a complexity in the
final product that synthetic feeds don't replicate. This is debated,
but the "living soil" community is passionate about it.

### The downsides

- **Slowest growth.** Organic nutrient delivery is inherently slower
  than dissolved synthetic salts in water. Plants in soil typically take
  1–2 weeks longer in veg to reach the same size as coco or hydro
- **Pest habitat.** Soil provides a home for fungus gnats, root aphids,
  and other soil-dwelling pests. Coco has some of this risk; hydro has
  almost none
- **Bulk and weight.** Soil is heavy. A 5-gallon fabric pot of wet soil
  weighs 30+ pounds. Moving plants is a workout

| Product | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Fox Farms Happy Frog | Mild, seedling-safe, good for starting in final pot |
| Fox Farms Ocean Forest | Hotter (more nutrients). Can burn seedlings. Best for transplanting into at week 3+ |

**Input: 6.0–6.8. Optimal: 6.3–6.5.**

## Coco coir: the fast middle ground

### What it is

Coco coir is the processed fiber from coconut husks. It's an inert
medium — it contains essentially no nutrients. You provide everything
through your nutrient solution, giving you complete control over what
the plant eats.

Coco looks and feels similar to soil but behaves differently. It holds
water and air simultaneously (excellent drainage + moisture retention),
allows faster root growth than soil, and supports faster nutrient
uptake because nutrients are dissolved and immediately available.

### Why growers choose coco

**Speed:** coco grows are typically 10–20% faster than soil grows
because nutrients are delivered in plant-available form — no waiting
for microbial breakdown. A strain that takes 4 weeks of veg in soil
might be ready in 3–3.5 weeks in coco.

**Control:** you know exactly what the plant is eating because you put
it there. No guessing about what the soil is releasing. This makes
troubleshooting easier — if there's a deficiency, it's because the
nutrient solution is missing it, not because soil biology failed to
release it.

**Reusable:** coco can be rinsed, re-buffered, and reused for multiple
grows. Soil needs to be replenished or replaced.

### The downsides

- **Cal-mag dependency.** Coco's cation exchange sites preferentially
  bind calcium and magnesium. If you don't supplement cal-mag in every
  feed, the coco steals Ca/Mg from your solution and your plants show
  deficiency even at correct pH
- **More frequent watering.** Coco dries faster than soil. In flower,
  daily watering (or even twice daily) is common. Automated watering
  becomes almost necessary for convenience
- **Less forgiving.** Without soil's buffering, pH and nutrient errors
  affect the plant within days instead of weeks

### Getting started with coco

1. Buy pre-washed, pre-buffered coco (Mother Earth, Canna Coco,
   Botanicare Cocogro). Un-buffered coco straight from the brick
   needs soaking and cal-mag charging before use
2. Mix with 25–30% perlite for drainage
3. Use a coco-specific nutrient line (Canna Coco A+B, General
   Hydroponics FloraCoco) — these include extra calcium and magnesium
4. Add supplemental cal-mag (1–2 mL/L) to every feed
5. pH every feeding to 5.8–6.0

For a complete coco guide, see our
[coco coir mastery guide](/guides/coco-coir-growing).

### pH targets for coco

**Input: 5.5–6.3. Optimal: 5.8–6.0.**

## Hydroponics (DWC): maximum growth

### What it is

Deep Water Culture (DWC) is the most common hydroponic method for home
cannabis growers. The plant sits in a net pot filled with clay pebbles
(hydroton), and the roots grow directly into a reservoir of oxygenated
nutrient solution. Air stones provide oxygen; the roots absorb nutrients
directly from the water.

### Why growers choose hydro

**Speed:** nothing grows cannabis faster than DWC. Roots have unlimited
access to dissolved nutrients and oxygen. Vegetative growth rates can
be 30–50% faster than soil. A plant that takes 4 weeks of veg in soil
might be ready in 2.5 weeks in DWC.

**Yield:** the increased growth rate translates to larger plants and
higher yields per cycle. Experienced DWC growers consistently report
20–30% higher yields than the same genetics in soil, given the same
light and space.

**Clean:** no soil means no soil-dwelling pests (fungus gnats, root
aphids). The grow is cleaner and the root health is directly visible.

### The downsides

- **Zero forgiveness.** The reservoir IS the root zone. pH swings hit
  roots immediately. Nutrient errors are felt within hours. A pump
  failure or air stone failure can kill roots in a day
- **Daily maintenance.** pH and EC must be checked daily. Water level,
  temperature, and dissolved oxygen require monitoring. This is a
  commitment
- **Equipment cost.** Buckets, net pots, air pumps, air stones, EC/pH
  meters, and potentially a water chiller add up. DWC startup is
  $100–200 more than soil
- **Root rot risk.** Warm water (above 72°F) with low dissolved oxygen
  breeds Pythium (root rot). A water chiller ($150–300) or strict
  beneficial bacteria regimen is often necessary

## The decision: which medium should you choose?

### First grow, just want results

**→ Soil.** Specifically, a pre-amended soil like Fox Farms Happy Frog
or Build-a-Soil 3.0. It forgives mistakes, requires less equipment,
and lets you focus on learning the plant instead of managing chemistry.

### Second or third grow, want faster results

**→ Coco coir.** You've learned the basics in soil. Now you want more
control and faster growth. Coco gives you that without the daily
maintenance commitment of hydro.

### Experienced grower, maximizing yield

**→ DWC.** You understand pH, EC, nutrient ratios, and plant signals.
You're willing to check the reservoir daily. DWC rewards the attentive
grower with the fastest growth and highest yields in home cultivation.

### Want the best-tasting flower and don't mind waiting

**→ Living soil / organic soil.** Build a super soil, add water, let
the soil biology feed the plant. Slower growth, but many connoisseurs
believe it produces the most complex terpene profiles and smoothest
smoke. See our [living soil guide](/guides/living-soil-no-till).

### Apartment grower, limited space, need simplicity

**→ Coco or soil in small pots.** Both work in 2–3 gallon pots for
micro grows. Coco is faster; soil is simpler. Choose based on how
often you can check on the plants. See our
[micro growing guide](/guides/micro-growing).

## Can you switch mid-grow?

Not really. Transplanting from soil to coco or vice versa is possible
but traumatic for the plant. Switching from soil/coco to hydro requires
washing all soil from the roots, which is extremely stressful and often
kills the plant. The time to choose your medium is before germination.

You can, however, start in one medium and switch for your next grow.
Many growers do their first run in soil, then switch to coco for
their second. The transition is easy because the growing techniques
are similar — only the watering frequency and nutrient management
differ.
